Week theme

Reset and re-anchor

Movement first. Contact point. Rhythm. After a busy April, today is about feeling the basics again — no pressure points, lots of consistency, lots of yes-moments.

Coaching rule

Drill duration matches the child's age in minutes

Younger kids' working attention span is short. Cross the limit and you lose them — stay under it and the same kid stays engaged for 90 minutes. Switch activity, switch game, switch constraint. Variety beats volume.

"Make the ball go high enough to touch the sky before it comes back."
Staged progression — earn the next step before climbing.

"Catch it to the side — like a real tennis shot!"
Contact point and spacing using a second racket as a training aid.
Three skills

Week 1 of 5 — Reset and re-anchor. Lots of "good… good…" between feeds. Make every kid leave saying "that was fun" so they show up Wednesday ready to work.

If a kid is struggling: regress one step — drop the racket, use hands, slow it down. Win in the simpler version, then climb back up.

If a kid is bored: add a constraint — non-dominant hand, alternate feet, a target. Boredom = under-challenged.

Send-off line: "See you Wednesday — we're going to start aiming for targets next time."
