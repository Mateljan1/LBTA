1 · Rainbow Toss + Foot Sliders (paired warm-up)
"Keep your eyes up and watch the ball the whole way — like tracking a shooting star."
Overhead tracking + multi-directional coordination. Warm-up that doubles as athletic prep.
First 5 min — Rainbow Toss
Players in pairs. Toss ball hand to hand in a rainbow arc (like a hook-shot), tracking with eyes the whole way. Both dominant and non-dominant hand.
Second 5 min — Foot Sliders
Each player has ball on ground + racket in hand. Hop on one foot while using the OTHER foot to push and roll the ball forward. Switch legs. Tougher: balance a ball on strings simultaneously.

2 · Mini Ball Rally
"Catch it to the side — throw it back from the side. Rotate your whole body."
Teaches rotation and spacing before they pick up a racket today. They often execute tactics with this ball before they can with a racket.
How
Pairs using a small basketball or similar-sized ball. Throw back and forth like forehand and backhand. Catch to SIDE of body, throw back with rotational motion. After 5 minutes: move each other around the court — start teaching pattern play.

3 · Tap and Hit
"Soften your hands — feel the ball on your strings, then send it."
Touch and feel. Forces them to absorb pace before hitting — foundation of soft-ball play.
How
Two players rally from baseline. Before hitting rally ball over, player must first tap the ball up to themselves (self-rally once), let it bounce, then hit to partner. Variation after 5 min: Tap forehand, hit backhand (or vice versa).

4 · Up – Back – Stay
"Read the ball before it bounces — call it as early as you can."
Anticipation. Single biggest separator between plateaued and improving players.
How
Two players cooperatively rally from baseline. Each strike, player must audibly call: UP (moving forward), BACK (moving back), or STAY (holding ground). Goal: call it before the ball bounces on your side. Make them yell it loud.

5 · Hot Lava
"Keep your toes on the line — if the ball is coming high, shorten your backswing."
Forces compact swing mechanics. Teaches them to take the ball earlier — a key competitive skill.
How
60-foot orange court. Both feet must stay inside the baseline at all times. Rally as normal. Stepping behind baseline = "hot lava" = lose the point.

4:50 – 5:0010 min
6 · Davis Cup Volleys
"Steer the ball — don't swing at it. Guide it with the bottom edge."
Volley control is harder than volley power. This makes that clear immediately.
How
Two players very close to net. Cooperatively volley back and forth while side-shuffling across the net and back. Control over power. As mastery grows, increase distance from net. Start with foam/low-compression ball.

5:00 – 5:1010 min
7 · Short or Deep
"Watch the arc — does it look like it's dropping fast or floating far?"
Court awareness + anticipation. Players who read depth get to the ball earlier, always.
How
Two players cooperatively rally from baseline. Players call SHORT (lands inside service line) or DEEP (past service line) for every incoming ball. Goal: call it before the ball bounces.
10:00
5:10 – 5:2515 min
8 · Capture the Net
"Win the point, then sprint — the net is the prize."
Teaches net transition as a reward, not a risk. The point-play finale.
How
Balls on ground at net both sides. Two players from baseline. You feed the first ball; point played out from baseline. Winner sprints to net, grabs a ball from the ground, bounce-feeds to opponent. Net winner stays and keeps feeding. Net loser retreats; opponent rushes to feed their own ball before retreating player is ready.

1 · Cardio Mini Tennis
"Sprint to the angle — first one there has the advantage."
Touch + fitness warm-up that wakes up the legs and the hands at once.
How
Teams of 2. Players start on singles sideline opposite each other. Feed a sharp angle. All four players run into position and play out a mini-tennis point. After 2 points, reset to other singles sideline. Feed again. Volleys allowed or not on your call.

2 · Up & Back Game
"Adjust your swing as you move — shorter when you're at the net, longer when you're back."
Especially helpful for adults stuck in no-man's land. Reset week — makes the transition zone feel natural again.
How
Two players, start at net facing each other. Cooperatively rally while slowly moving back to baseline, then forward again. Use just 1 ball if possible. Hit volleys, half-volleys, no-man's land shots, groundstrokes throughout. Error → restart from where you are.

3 · Far Side Cross-Court
"Every ball goes to the same place — make it deep and give yourself margin."
Counter-intuitive result — the restricted player (crosscourt only) often wins more points. Powerful Week 1 lesson on high-percentage tennis.
How
Two players baseline. Far side player (away from you) may ONLY hit crosscourt. Near side player may hit anywhere. Switch ends after 8-10 min. Mid-block constraint: increase target depth — crosscourt ball must land behind service line to count.

4 · Cardio Doubles
"Jog to position — if you're walking, you're resting when you should be moving."
Fitness assessment disguised as a doubles game. Reveals who falls apart in a long third set.
How
Regular doubles. Time between points spent jogging into position for next point. 3–5 seconds max between points. Can complete an entire set in 10–12 minutes. Rotate partners every set.

5 · The Big Mo (momentum scoring)
"String two together — the second point is where the score is."
Adults have the hardest time stringing points. This makes the limitation visible and gives them a reason to develop between-point routines.
How
Two games on half-courts simultaneously. Win 1 point in a row = 0 points scored. Win 2 in a row = 1 point. Win 3+ in a row = 1 point each additional.
