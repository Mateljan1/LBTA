One of the most important things we do happens before enrollment: placement.

We don't sort players by age alone. We look at:

  • Experience level and stroke development
  • Rally consistency and movement quality
  • Goals—recreational, competitive, team-based, or fitness-focused
  • Confidence and readiness

Why this matters:

  • Faster progress (right level = right challenge)
  • Better enjoyment (especially for kids)
  • Stronger retention (players stay because they're growing)

This applies whether you're considering:

  • A youth development program for your child
  • Junior Team Tennis for a competitive junior
  • Adult classes for yourself

LBTA Insight

The "Two Levels" Mistake

One of the most common mistakes in tennis development? Placing a player in a group that's too advanced. It feels flattering—but it backfires. Players who are slightly challenged improve. Players who are overwhelmed lose confidence and quit. Proper placement isn't about ego. It's about growth.

If you're unsure where you or your child fits, that's exactly what we're here to help with.
