What is JTT?

Junior Team Tennis (JTT) is a USTA-sanctioned league where players train together during the week and compete as a team on weekends against other clubs and academies across Orange County.

Think of it as the youth soccer or Little League of tennis — but with a focus on individual skill development alongside team competition.

How It Works:

  • Weekday Practices: 3× per week (Mon/Wed/Fri), 2 hours each
  • Weekend Matches: Saturdays or Sundays against other teams
  • Team Format: Players compete in singles and doubles matches
  • Coach Support: LBTA coaches at every practice and match
  • Season Length: 15 weeks (Jan 12 - Apr 26, 2026)

Why JTT Matters

JTT isn't just about winning matches. It's about developing complete players who understand competition, teamwork, and resilience.

Match Experience Under Pressure

Private lessons teach technique. JTT teaches how to use that technique when it matters — during real matches with teammates counting on you.

Team Accountability

When your teammates are depending on you, you show up. You practice harder. You focus better. That's the power of belonging to a team.

Friendships That Last

The bonds formed during JTT season — celebrating wins, supporting each other through losses — create friendships that extend beyond tennis.

What to Expect

During Practices:

  • Structured warm-ups and movement training
  • Technical development (strokes, serves, volleys)
  • Tactical drills (patterns, positioning, strategy)
  • Match-play scenarios and point construction
  • Team bonding and competitive mindset building

On Match Days:

  • Arrive 30 minutes early for team warm-up
  • Coach assigns match order (singles/doubles)
  • Play 2-3 matches per player (depending on division)
  • Coaches provide strategy and support between matches
  • Team celebrates wins, learns from losses together

Is JTT Right for Your Player?

  • Enjoys competition and wants more match experience
  • Thrives with structure and accountability
  • Wants to be part of a team (not just individual lessons)

Common Questions

Q: Does my child need to have played JTT before?

No! We have first-time JTT players every season. Coaches assess everyone at the first practice and make sure they're in the right division.

Q: What if they can't make every practice?

They should attend at least 2 out of 3 practices per week. Occasional absences are fine — we understand school and family commitments.

Q: How competitive is it?

Within each division, teams are placed in Gold, Silver, or Bronze brackets based on skill level. Everyone competes at the right level — no one gets overwhelmed or under-challenged.

Q: What if my child is nervous about competing?

That's exactly why JTT exists. The team environment makes competition less intimidating. They're not alone — they have teammates and coaches supporting them every step.
