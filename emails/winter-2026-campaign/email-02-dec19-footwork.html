The Split-Step (Your Secret Weapon)

Watch Federer. Watch Nadal. Watch any pro.

Right before their opponent hits, they do a small hop.

It's called a split-step, and it's the single most important footwork move in tennis.

Why it works:

•   Loads your legs (like coiling a spring)
•   Puts you on your toes (ready to move any direction)
•   Creates rhythm (no more flat-footed reaching)

How to practice:

1.   Hop lightly as the ball leaves your opponent's racquet
2.   Land on your toes (not heels)
3.   Immediately push off toward the ball

Do this 1,000 times and your tennis changes overnight.
