The 3 Grips (And What They Mean)

1. The "Hammer Grip" (Wrong)

Holding racquet like a hammer. Fist clenched.

What it means:

•  New player (totally normal!)
•  Trying too hard (relax!)
•  Hitting flat (no spin possible)

Fix: Eastern grip (shake hands with racquet)

2. The "Continental Grip" (Versatile)

Like holding a hammer, but rotated.

What it means:

•  You played in the 80s-90s (old school!)
•  Good for serves and volleys
•  Struggles with modern topspin baseline game

Use for: Serves, volleys, slice

Not for: Heavy topspin groundstrokes

3. The "Eastern/Semi-Western Grip" (Modern)

Palm more behind the racquet handle.

What it means:

•  You learned proper modern technique
•  Can generate topspin easily
•  Comfortable hitting high balls

Use for: Forehands, topspin groundstrokes

Best for: Most players today

 

Why Grip Matters

Wrong grip = You'll never generate proper spin or power.

Right grip = Technique flows naturally.

It's the foundation of everything. We check grips in week 1 of every program. If it's wrong, we fix it immediately.
