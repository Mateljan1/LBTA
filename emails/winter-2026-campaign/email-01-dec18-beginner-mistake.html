Quick question: What's the first thing most beginners do wrong when they start tennis?

They grip the racquet like a hammer and swing with all their strength.

I see it every single first lesson.

Here's what I tell them:

"Tennis is chess with a racquet. Power comes later. Control comes first."

The players who succeed? They embrace the basics:

•   Proper grip (Eastern forehand - like shaking hands with the racquet)
•   Unit turn (shoulders rotate before the swing starts)
•   Contact point (out in front, not beside your body)
•   Follow-through (finish high for topspin)

Boring? Maybe.

Effective? Always.
