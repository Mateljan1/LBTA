The Serve Power Myth

Common Belief

Swing arm faster = more power

Reality

Use your legs + body rotation = more power (and less shoulder pain)

 

The 3 Serve Power Sources (In Order)

1.

Legs (40% of power)

→Bend knees
→Push up as you make contact
→Like jumping into the serve
2.

Body Rotation (40% of power)

→Coil your shoulders (like a spring)
→Uncoil as you swing
→Hips lead, shoulders follow
3.

Arm (20% of power)

→Relaxed arm (not tense)
→Fast racquet head (wrist snap)
→Clean contact (middle of strings)

Total = Fast serve without arm strain

 

Why This Matters

I see adults hurt their shoulder every season.

Why? They're all arm on the serve.

The players who serve pain-free and powerful? They use their whole body.

This is especially important for:

• Adults returning after years off (prevent injury)
• Juniors building tournament serves (proper mechanics early)
• Competitive players (add 10-20mph immediately)
 

How We Teach Serves at LBTA

We spend time on serves. A lot of time.

Because a good serve:

✓ Starts every point with confidence
✓ Prevents you from being defensive
✓ Intimidates opponents (even recreationally)

Our progression:

Weeks 1-2: Toss consistency (if you can't toss, you can't serve)
Weeks 3-4: Leg drive (using lower body)
Weeks 5-6: Body rotation (coiling/uncoiling)
Weeks 7+: Racquet speed (relaxed arm whip)

By week 13, your serve is unrecognizable.
