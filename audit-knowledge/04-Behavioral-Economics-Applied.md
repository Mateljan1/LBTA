# 04 - BEHAVIORAL ECONOMICS APPLIED TO WEB

## 10 Behavioral Economics Principles for Conversion

---

## 1. ANCHORING EFFECT
**Definition:** First number seen disproportionately influences perceived value

### Website Applications
- **Pricing anchor:** Show highest price first, then highlight discount
  - "Was $2,000 → Now $997" (not just "$997")
- **Savings anchor:** "$1,000 value → Pay $297" (anchors perception at $1,000)
- **Time anchor:** "2-hour benefit for 30 minutes of work"

### Implementation
```
❌ Just show: "$297"
✅ Show: "Full coaching normally $2,000 per month. Join our community program for $297."
```

Revenue impact: +15-25% average from anchoring.

---

## 2. DECOY EFFECT
**Definition:** Three options (with one clearly inferior) influence choice toward optimal middle

### Website Applications
- **Three-tier pricing:**
  - Basic: $97/month
  - Pro: $297/month (psychologically optimal)
  - Premium: $497/month
  - The decoy is "Basic" - makes Pro seem like best value

### The Science
- Two options: Customer torn between low/high
- Three options: Middle option becomes psychologically "optimal"
- Decoy must be clearly inferior (not better than middle)

### Implementation
```
Pricing without decoy:
- Starter: $97 (too cheap, perceived as low-quality)
- Professional: $297 (too expensive, no comparison)

With decoy (optimal):
- Starter: $97 (clearly inferior, decoy)
- Professional: $297 (appears optimal, psychological sweet spot)
- Premium: $497 (makes Professional look like better value)

Revenue impact: +20-40% by adding decoy tier.
```

---

## 3. LOSS AVERSION
**Definition:** Fear of losing something > excitement of gaining it

### Website Applications
- **Messaging reframe:**
  - ❌ "Gain $50K in revenue"
  - ✅ "Stop leaving $50K/year on the table"

- **Scarcity:**
  - ❌ "Limited spots available"
  - ✅ "Only 5 spots left before next cohort in 6 weeks"

- **Urgency:**
  - ❌ "Sign up soon"
  - ✅ "Prices increase January 1st"

### Implementation
- Use "Stop/Avoid/Don't miss" language
- Emphasize what they lose by not buying
- Make deadlines specific and real

Revenue impact: +10-15% from messaging reframe alone.

---

## 4. SOCIAL PROOF (PEER CONFORMITY)
**Definition:** People make decisions based on what others do

### Hierarchy of Proof Strength
1. **Founder/Expert:** "USTA Professional with 20 years experience" (strongest)
2. **Third-party expert:** "Recommended by Coach X, Tennis Magazine" (strong)
3. **Peer testimonial:** "John Smith improved his serve 10 mph" (moderate)
4. **Crowd metrics:** "5,000+ customers" (weakest alone)

### Website Applications
- **Lead with expert proof:**
  - Founder photo + credentials first
  - Third-party endorsements second
  - Then peer testimonials
  - Finally crowd metrics

- **Specificity matters:**
  - ❌ "Great results!"
  - ✅ "Improved serve speed from 95 to 110 mph in 8 weeks"

- **Recency matters:**
  - Recent testimonials (< 6 months) more credible
  - Include date on testimonial

### Implementation
```
✅ "Sarah Chen improved from 4.0 to 5.5 rating in 12 weeks
   Completed in November 2024
   'The personalized coaching completely changed my game'"

❌ "Great testimonial! Someone loved our service"
```

Revenue impact: +20-30% improvement from specific social proof.

---

## 5. DEFAULT BIAS
**Definition:** People accept default option without considering alternatives

### Website Applications
- **Pricing default:** Default to most profitable option (not cheapest)
  - If Pro is most profitable, make it pre-selected
  - Justified: "Most popular" or "Recommended"

- **Frequency default:**
  - Annual billing pre-selected (saves you money vs monthly)
  - Monthly pre-selected if annual conflicts with affordability goal

- **Quantity default:**
  - Pre-select recommended package quantity

### Implementation
```
❌ No default selected, customer must choose
✅ "Most Popular" badge + pre-selected for Pro tier

Result: 30-50% of customers accept default without changing
```

Revenue impact: +15-25% from optimal default selection.

---

## 6. SCARCITY PRINCIPLE
**Definition:** Limited availability increases perceived value

### IMPORTANT: Only Real Scarcity Works
- ❌ Fake countdown timers = damage trust
- ✅ Real scarcity = builds trust

### Website Applications
- **Cohort-based scarcity:**
  - "6 spots for December cohort"
  - "Next cohort starts January 15"
  - Real, verifiable scarcity

- **Time-based scarcity:**
  - "Early bird pricing ends Friday"
  - Price increases after deadline
  - Real deadline, not artificial

- **Inventory scarcity:**
  - "Only 3 remaining" (if true)
  - Real stock limit

### Implementation
```
✅ "December cohort: 6 spots, 4 remaining. Next cohort starts Jan 15."
❌ "Limited time offer!" (with countdown timer)
```

Revenue impact: +10-20% from real scarcity messaging.

---

## 7. CONSISTENCY/COMMITMENT PRINCIPLE
**Definition:** Small initial commitments lead to larger ones

### Website Applications
- **Funnel progression:**
  - Free assessment → $97 course → $297 program → $2,000 1-on-1
  - Each step builds commitment

- **Free trial value:**
  - Give substantial free value (week of access, full assessment)
  - Creates reciprocity obligation

- **Progressive commitment:**
  - First step is low-risk commitment
  - Then upsell to larger commitment

### Implementation
```
Week 1: Free assessment (builds commitment to improvement)
Week 2: Free lesson access (builds knowledge of method)
Week 3: Introduce $97 course (natural next step)
Month 2: Introduce $297 program (level-up message)
Month 3: Introduce premium coaching (elite option)
```

Revenue impact: +5-10% per commitment step; compounding effect.

---

## 8. AUTHORITY BIAS
**Definition:** We trust and comply with authority figures

### Website Applications
- **Founder visibility:**
  - Photo, full name, credentials
  - ❌ Anonymous expert
  - ✅ "Coach Mike Chen, USTA Certified, 15 years experience"

- **Credentials emphasis:**
  - Certifications displayed
  - Educational background visible
  - Third-party endorsements prominent

- **Expert positioning:**
  - Lead with expertise (not price)
  - Establish credibility first

### Implementation
```
❌ "Join our tennis training program"
✅ "USTA-certified coach with 2,000+ successful students teaches tennis strategy and mechanics"
```

Revenue impact: +15-25% from authority positioning.

---

## 9. RECIPROCITY PRINCIPLE
**Definition:** If you give first, people feel obligated to give back

### Website Applications
- **Free audit/assessment:**
  - Substantial free value (40-60 minute assessment)
  - Creates reciprocity obligation
  - Natural upsell to paid program

- **Free checklist:**
  - Comprehensive, genuinely useful checklist
  - Demonstrates expertise
  - Naturally leads to premium offer

- **Free lesson:**
  - Full lesson (not snippet)
  - Shows quality of instruction
  - Builds want for more

### Implementation
```
Lead magnet: "Complete 50-point Tennis Improvement Checklist"
(Delivered via email, genuinely useful, demonstrates expertise)

Email sequence:
Day 1: Here's your checklist
Day 2: How to use checklist
Day 3: Introduce paid program (natural upsell)
```

Revenue impact: +20-30% for properly leveraged reciprocity.

---

## 10. AVAILABILITY HEURISTIC
**Definition:** Information that comes to mind easily feels more likely

### Website Applications
- **Key benefit repetition:**
  - Repeat primary benefit 3-5 times on page
  - Headline, subheading, body, testimonial, CTA
  - Makes benefit more "available" in customer mind

- **Social proof frequency:**
  - Repeat customer count multiple times
  - Mentions on multiple pages
  - Feels more credible through repetition

### Implementation
```
Page structure showing benefit repetition:
- H1: "Improve Tennis Ranking 2 Points in 12 Weeks"
- Subheading: "Specific training to reach your ranking goal"
- Body: "Our method improves ranking through..."
- Testimonial: "I improved my ranking from 4.0 to 6.0"
- CTA: "Start Improving Your Ranking Today"

Result: Benefit is "top of mind" through repetition
```

Revenue impact: +10-15% from message availability.
