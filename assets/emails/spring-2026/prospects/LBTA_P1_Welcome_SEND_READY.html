A FRAMEWORK FROM ANDREW — TRAFFIC LIGHT ZONES

Every ball coming at you is one of three colors.

Red — you’re stretched, late, or deep behind the baseline. This is not the time to go for a winner. Hit it high over the net, deep to the middle. Buy time. Recover your position.

Yellow — it’s a neutral ball. Neither player has the advantage. Hit to the biggest target — usually big crosscourt. Stay patient. Wait for something better.

Green — you got the short ball. Feet are set. You’re balanced. Now you attack — but to the open space, not by swinging harder.

Most errors happen when players treat a red ball like a green one. They’re on the run, feet aren’t set, and they try to hit a winner. The shot was fine. The decision was wrong.

Try it: Next time you play, before every shot, ask: red, yellow, or green? Just that one question. You’ll be surprised how many errors disappear when the decision matches the situation.
