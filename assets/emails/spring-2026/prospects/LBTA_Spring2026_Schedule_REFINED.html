Spring 2026 at LBTA: three locations, programs for every level, and a system that works. Below you’ll find the complete schedule organized by location — your court, your time, your pathway forward.

Every program follows a structured curriculum with weekly themes, measurable goals, and progressions that build session to session. This isn’t drop-in ball-hitting. It’s a development system where fun comes first, structure creates confidence, and every player follows a real pathway. We’ve coached players from their first forehand to Division I college tennis and to the professional tour. The system works because it’s built on movement, craft, and community — not just drills.

Our coaches use constraints-led coaching — carefully designed games and rules that shape smarter decisions on court without over-coaching. The game teaches. The coach guides. The player owns the process. This is how Grand Slam players train. This is how we train your player.

Coach-to-player ratios are capped at every level (listed below). If enrollment exceeds the ratio, we bring in an additional coach — quality never gets diluted. You’re investing in real development, not a crowded court.

Coach’s Tip from Andrew Mateljan

“Big Target First.”

This is one of our core decision rules and it works for every level from age 5 to adult: in neutral rallies, aim to the biggest target first — usually the middle or big crosscourt. Most points are lost, not won. The player who sends one more ball to a safe target beats the one painting lines. Next time you play, commit to hitting every first ball crosscourt. You’ll win more points just by starting smart.

— Andrew Mateljan, Founder & Academy Director
