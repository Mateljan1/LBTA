A NOTE FROM ANDREW

Read the Ball Like a Traffic Light.

The decision framework we teach at every level — and one question to try this week

Hey %FIRSTNAME%,

Here’s something I see on the courts every single week.

A player gets pushed deep behind the baseline. Feet aren’t set. Weight is moving backward. And instead of playing a safe, high ball back to the middle of the court — buying themselves time to recover — they try to rip a winner.

It misses by three inches. They shake their head. “I need to fix my forehand.”

But the forehand was fine. The decision was wrong. They were playing defense and chose an offensive shot.

At LBTA, we teach every player — from age 9 to adult — to read each ball like a traffic light.

THE LBTA FRAMEWORK — TRAFFIC LIGHT ZONES

Red ball — you’re stretched, late, or deep behind the baseline. Go high over the net, deep to the middle. Buy time. Recover. That’s all.

Yellow ball — it’s neutral. Neither player has the advantage. Hit to the biggest target — big crosscourt, through the middle. Stay patient. Wait for something better.

Green ball — you got the short ball. Your feet are set. You’re balanced. Now you attack — but to the open space, not by swinging harder.

Try it this week: Next time you play, ask yourself one question before every shot: red, yellow, or green? Count how many times you were playing green when the ball was red. That’s where your errors live.
