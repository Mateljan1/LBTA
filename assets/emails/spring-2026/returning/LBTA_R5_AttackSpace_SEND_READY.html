THE LBTA FRAMEWORK — ATTACK = SPACE, NOT SPEED

When most players get a short ball, their instinct is to swing harder. It feels right. It’s almost always wrong.

You tighten up. Your margin shrinks. The ball sails long or catches the tape. You had the opportunity and you blew it — not because of your technique, but because you confused attacking with swinging hard.

Watch any professional on a short ball. They move forward, stay smooth, and place it to the open side of the court. Same swing speed. Better result. The attack is in the placement, not the power.

The rule: when you get a green light ball (short, hittable, feet set), find the open space and hit to it at 70% pace. Smooth to the target. Finish balanced.

Try it this week: Next time you get a short ball, resist the urge to swing harder. Step in, aim for the open court, and hit it at the same speed as your rally ball. Notice how much more often it goes in — and how much more pressure it puts on your opponent.
