Summer registration is open — and I want to help you plan it well, not just sign up for everything.

There’s a principle in training design called acute-to-chronic load ratio. The short version: large week-to-week spikes in volume — going from one hour of tennis to twenty hours overnight — increase fatigue, reduce quality, and raise injury risk. This applies to adults and juniors alike.

The best summer plan isn’t “max out every week.” It’s a combination of concentrated volume (camp weeks) and steady consistency (weekly classes).

Camp weeks give your player 12+ hours in four days — the kind of focused immersion where skills click. Weekly classes maintain the rhythm between camps so those gains don’t evaporate. One without the other doesn’t work as well.

A note on this summer specifically: the pool at Alta Laguna is closed, so there’s no Swim & Tennis this year. Camp is all tennis plus grass field games, water activities on Tuesdays and Thursdays, arts & crafts, playground time, and pickleball. Kids bring lunch. Full-day options available.
