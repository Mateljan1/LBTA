UTR Explained
Universal Tennis Rating.

UTR is the global standard for measuring tennis ability. Used by NCAA programs, professional coaches, and junior academies worldwide, it gives every player a single number on a 1–16 scale that accurately reflects their current level.

1.0 – 3.0Beginner
3.0 – 5.0Intermediate
5.0 – 8.0Advanced
8.0 – 12.0Elite
12.0 – 16.0Professional
How Your Rating Is Calculated
Who you played — Beating a higher-rated opponent moves your rating more.
Score margin — A 6-1, 6-1 win moves it differently than 7-6, 7-6.
Recency — Recent matches carry more weight than older results.
Don’t have a UTR yet?
No problem. If you’re new to UTR we’ll help you get your initial rating before the season starts. Your first few circuit matches establish a baseline — from there your rating moves based on results. For Color Ball players, we’ll place you based on your USTA ball stage.

Questions & Registration
Good questions.
Do I need a UTR rating to register?
No. If you’re new to UTR we’ll help you get your initial rating before the season starts. Your first few matches establish a baseline.
Is my child ready for Color Ball?
If they’re working through the ball progression — Red, Orange, or Green Ball — yes. Designed for younger players ready for structured competitive play.
Can adults and juniors compete together?
Yes. Divisions are level-based, not age-based. A junior with a 4.0 UTR competes alongside adults at the same level.
What if I miss a Saturday?
Season players hold their spot for all 8 weeks. Let us know 24 hours in advance. Two consecutive no-shows without notice may affect your spot.
Can I register for more than one division?
Yes, if your rating qualifies. The Doubles Round Robin runs in the evening and is popular as a second session.
Who is Karue Sell?
Brazilian pro ranked as high as ATP No. 258. UCLA standout (104–31 career singles, two NCAA final fours). Former hitting partner for Naomi Osaka and coach to ATP pro Marcos Giron. Runs a 182K-subscriber YouTube channel documenting life on tour. He’ll be at Grand Finals for the pro exhibition and to play doubles with our Season Champion.
