What Is USTA League Tennis?
USTA leagues are the organized team competition system run by the United States Tennis Association. You join a team of 12–14 players at your skill level. Each week, your team plays a match against another Orange County club. You rotate in and out of the lineup so everyone gets fair court time. Think of it like a recreational sports league — except it’s tennis, it’s structured, and it’s tracked nationally through USTA.
What Match Day Looks Like
1
You get the lineup — Andrew sets the weekly lineup and sends it by text. You know your match, partner, and venue a few days ahead.
2
You show up and play — Arrive at the host club, warm up, and play your singles or doubles match. Matches take 60–90 minutes.
3
The team wins (or learns) — Win the majority of lines and your team wins the match. Results are reported to USTA for your record.

Not Sure of Your Level?

NTRP is how USTA measures your skill. Here’s a quick guide:

2.5
Can rally but inconsistent. Learning to serve. New to match play.
4.0
Strong all-court game. Spin, pace variation, tactical patterns.
3.0
Consistent rally. Developing serve. Playing points with intention.
4.5+
Tournament-level. Weapons on serve and groundstrokes. Advanced tactics.
3.5
Reliable groundstrokes and serve. Can place the ball. Comfortable in doubles.
