Coaching Staff

Andrew Mateljan
Founder & Academy Director | Head Coach, LBHS Boys Tennis

Andrew Mateljan is one of Southern California’s most respected player development coaches and the founder of Laguna Beach Tennis Academy — the official tennis partner of the City of Laguna Beach. With over 25 years in the sport as a nationally ranked junior competitor, ITF Futures professional, international touring coach, and academy director, Andrew has built a comprehensive development system serving nearly 2,000 families.

Playing Career
Ranked #3 in SoCal and #12 nationally by USTA. Won The Ojai and captured multiple SoCal Sectional titles. Competed twice at the USTA Boys National Championships in Kalamazoo. Competed on the ITF Futures circuit across the US, South America, and internationally, earning an ITF World Junior Ranking.
International Coaching
Seven years coaching internationally across Europe — Spain, Croatia, and Norway — training within boutique high-performance facilities and absorbing the European player development philosophy. GPTCA-ATP certified coach and featured speaker at the World Tennis Conference.

Coaching Staff

Peter DeFrantz
USPTA & PTR Certified Professional | College Tennis | 8+ Years Coaching

Peter DeFrantz grew up on the tennis court. Raised by a father who coached high school tennis, he learned early that the game is about more than strokes — it’s about how you carry yourself, how you compete, and how you treat the people around you.

He went on to play college tennis at Mt. San Jacinto College, where his team was ranked #1 in Southern California and #2 in the state. That competitive foundation — combined with dual USPTA and PTR certification — shapes how he coaches today: with structure, with intention, and with genuine care for every player he works with.

Over 8+ years of professional coaching across Southern California, Peter has built a reputation for making tennis accessible and engaging for players of all ages and abilities.

Coaching Strengths
Communication — Connects with children of all ages using language and examples they understand. Teaching Method — Progressive, scaffolded approach starting inside the service line, gradually expanding distance and complexity. Player Development — Strong observation and evaluation skills with constructive feedback. Fitness Integration — Passionate about on-court and off-court fitness. Energy & Attitude — Consistently positive presence; lessons are fun, challenging, and engaging.
