# Annual Strategy Review — 2 hr

> Once a year. End of Q1 (last week of March). Andrew + an advisor or trusted peer. The big zoom out: did the GPT system change the academy?

---

## Pre-work (90 min, the day before)

Pull these inputs:

1. **All 4 quarterly review docs** for the past year.
2. **`09-COMMAND-CENTER/roadmap.md`** with last year's plan.
3. **Annual academy metrics** — enrollments, revenue, retention, NPS.
4. **Hours saved per role over the year** (Andrew, Allison, Saska).
5. **What the team thinks** — 10-min interview with Allison and Saska in advance:
   - "What's something the GPT now does that you can't imagine doing without?"
   - "What's still annoying?"
   - "What's a thing the GPT *can't* do that you wish it could?"

## Agenda (2 hr)

### 0:00–0:20 — The big number

What's the single number that says whether this worked?

- **Andrew's hours/week back to coaching, not admin.**
- Was the goal hit? (If yes, by how much? If no, why?)

This is the headline. Everything else either supports it or doesn't.

### 0:20–0:40 — Compounded value, one year out

For each of Andrew, Allison, Saska:
- One workflow that didn't exist a year ago.
- One workflow that compounded (now 5x faster than year start).
- One workflow that the GPT killed (was burdensome, now gone).

Three things × three people = 9 specific changes. Write them down. They're proof.

### 0:40–1:00 — Identity check

The Curve 2 question: **Did becoming a GPT-leveraged academy change who we are?**

- Are we still the founder-led, craft-first academy?
- Did the GPTs let Andrew show up *more* on court, or *less*?
- Did Allison and Saska sound *more* themselves in writing this year, or *less*?
- Are parents and members noticing? (Good way? Bad way? Not at all?)

If the answer to the last 3 is "more themselves, not less," **the system worked.** If not, recalibrate.

### 1:00–1:20 — The next ambitious move

Looking forward 12 months, what's the next big leverage point?

Possible directions (pick 1, max 2):
- A 7th GPT for a new role (e.g. Player Development, tournament coordinator, parent education).
- An ActiveCampaign-deeper integration (auto-export, weekly digest).
- A "knowledge-grade" voice library — 200+ curated drafts.
- An LBTA-internal parent education GPT for member families.
- An audit-ready compliance layer if we ever have multiple sites.
- Sunsetting one of the six GPTs if it's not pulling weight.

Don't pick more than 2. Compound > optimization.

### 1:20–1:40 — What we will stop

Just as important. What is on the roadmap that's no longer worth doing?

- Capabilities the team didn't use.
- Knowledge files no one reads.
- Workflows that didn't fit the academy's rhythm.

Cutting is compounding.

### 1:40–2:00 — Write the year's narrative

Andrew writes a 1-page note in `09-COMMAND-CENTER/changelog.md`. Tone: founder voice. What changed. What we learned. What we're building toward.

This becomes part of the academy's documented history — readable a year later when planning the next strategy review.

## Output

- 1-page annual narrative in `changelog.md`.
- Updated `roadmap.md` for the year ahead — short, focused, 2–3 priorities max.
- Decisions on what to add, cut, or harden across all 6 GPTs.
- A clear answer to: **Is the academy more itself this year because of these tools, or less?**

## What this is NOT

- Not a victory lap (the GPTs are tools; they don't deserve credit, the team does).
- Not a doom session (don't catastrophize fragility — this is a tool, not a system of record).
- Not a roadmap workshop (limit to 2 priorities; resist scope creep).

## The test

If the annual review takes <2 hours, you're not zooming out enough.
If it takes >3 hours, you're trying to do the quarterly review here. Save it.
