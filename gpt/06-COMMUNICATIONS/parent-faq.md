# Parent FAQ — "Are you using AI?"

> When parents (or members) ask whether LBTA uses AI in its communications, here's the canonical answer. Calm, specific, no apology, no hype.

---

## The short answer (use this if a parent asks casually)

> "We use AI to draft some of our communications faster — we read every draft before it goes out, in our voice, and we never let AI handle anything emotional or about your child specifically. It saves us writing time so we can spend more time on the court."

## The longer answer (when a parent is concerned and wants more detail)

> "Good question. Here's how we use it.
>
> **What AI does for us:** drafts the routine emails — schedule confirmations, weekly group recaps, weather updates, FAQ replies. It's pre-loaded with our published programs, prices, and policies, so the facts are accurate. It's also tuned to sound like the coach who's sending it (Andrew, Allison, or Saska) — not generic.
>
> **What AI does NOT do for us:** it does not send anything automatically. Every email gets read by the coach before it's sent. It does not handle anything emotional, custodial, safety-related, or about your child's specific progress. Those messages are written by the coach, end-to-end.
>
> **What this means for you:** the routine logistics emails reach you faster and with fewer errors. The personal messages — the ones about your kid — are still 100% from us, the way they always were. We pick up the phone for the things that matter.
>
> If you'd ever prefer a human-only thread for any reason, just tell us and we'll honor it."

## The values frame (if asked "why")

> "Two reasons. First — it gives us more time on the court, which is what we're here to do. Second — it makes the routine stuff (schedules, prices, policies) less likely to be wrong. It's the right tool for that kind of work. We're careful not to use it for anything else."

## What we say if asked "Is this email AI-written?"

The honest answer:
> "[Yes / No, this one I wrote myself]. The AI drafts a starting point on the routine ones, and I edit before sending. The personal ones — like this one — I write myself."

Don't pretend AI wasn't involved on a routine email. Don't oversell that it was on a personal one. Honesty compounds trust.

## What we never say

- "An AI wrote it."  *(reductive — a coach reviewed and shipped it)*
- "AI handles all our communications now."  *(false and damaging)*
- "Don't worry about it, it's just a tool."  *(dismissive — the question is usually about trust)*
- "We're using ChatGPT."  *(off-brand specificity — feels like trade-press talk)*

## What to do if a parent prefers no-AI emails

Honor it. Add a tag to that family's notes (in PBP or a private doc): **"Prefers human-only comms."** Coaches write all their emails by hand. This is rare; it's not a burden.

## Internal — when to surface this conversation publicly

If we get >5 parent inquiries about AI usage in a quarter, **publish a short version of this on the website** under `/about` or `/faq`. Quiet transparency builds trust faster than silence.

## What this document is for

When Allison or Saska gets a question they don't have language for, they open this file and find the answer. It's the single source of truth for "how we talk about how we use AI."

If a parent question doesn't fit any of these scripts, route to Andrew. New patterns get added here.
