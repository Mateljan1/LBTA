# LBTA GPT — Team Activation Playbook

> How to actually get Andrew, Allison, and Saska using these GPTs every day.

---

## The honest problem

Deploying a GPT is the easy part. **Getting a coach to actually use it daily is the hard part.** Coaches are not writers. They're on courts. The first time they sit down and the GPT asks them a question instead of giving an answer, they will quietly stop using it.

This playbook is the activation engine. Run it for each person. Don't skip steps.

---

## Activation = 3 phases per person

### Phase 1 — The 20-minute first session (Andrew runs this with each coach, in person, on iPad or laptop)

**Goal:** They feel a "wow" moment in the first 5 minutes, ship one real reply they would have actually written today, and leave knowing exactly when to use it tomorrow.

**Script (20 min):**

1. **(2 min) The frame.** *"This is your second brain for parent emails. It already knows our pricing, schedule, every coach, every program. It will draft replies in your voice. You read, edit one line if needed, send. Goal: save you 30 minutes a day."*

2. **(3 min) The first try — easy win.** Pull a real parent email from their inbox. Paste it. Hit one of the conversation starters: *"Draft a reply to this parent email in my voice."* Watch them read the draft. Ask: *"How close is that to what you would have written?"* Goal: they say "really close." Edit one line live, send.

3. **(5 min) The second try — a known-tough scenario.** Pick something they actually struggle with. For Allison: a USTA lineup conflict. For Saska: a parent asking "is my kid good enough to compete?" Watch the GPT handle it. Note where it routes vs answers.

4. **(5 min) The "kill switch" moment.** Show them a question the GPT *should not answer.* For Saska: a custody dispute message. Watch the GPT correctly draft a holding reply and flag for Andrew. **This is the trust moment.** They learn the GPT knows what *not* to do.

5. **(3 min) Their daily trigger.** Decide together: *when in your day will you reach for this?* Examples:
   - Allison: "Every morning before coffee, I open Founder's GPT and triage my inbox."
   - Saska: "Every Friday at 4pm, I draft the weekly parent recap."
   - Andrew: "Every email that takes >2 minutes to think about."

6. **(2 min) The compound promise.** *"Every Friday at 11am we do a 30-minute review. You bring one moment it nailed and one moment it missed. We update the system together. By month 3, this thing will know you better than your last assistant did."*

**Output:** Coach has 1 real shipped email and 1 daily trigger. They will be back tomorrow.

---

### Phase 2 — The 7-day ramp (each coach, post-onboarding)

**Goal:** Move from "I tried it once" to "this is in my daily routine."

**Day 1:** Use the 4 conversation starters in order, on real inputs. Log results in dogfood log.
**Day 2:** Use the GPT for at least one real parent or student email. Don't write it yourself first.
**Day 3:** Try the "kill switch." Ask the GPT something out of scope. Confirm it routes correctly.
**Day 4:** Use it for a longer-form draft (Saska: weekly summary. Allison: team update. Andrew: strategic decision).
**Day 5:** Notice a miss. Log it. Don't fix yet — just observe.
**Day 6:** Notice a "wow." Log it. Tell the team.
**Day 7:** Show up to the Friday Compound Review with both notes ready.

**Andrew's job during Phase 2:** Slack each coach once per day with a low-pressure check-in: *"Used it today? Anything weird?"* The first 7 days is where adoption lives or dies. Be in their corner.

---

### Phase 3 — The 30-day habit (after the 7-day ramp)

**Goal:** The GPT becomes invisible — the way email or PlayByPoint is invisible. They reach for it without thinking.

**Andrew's check-in cadence drops:** Slack ping every 3 days, then weekly.

**Habit-forming triggers (built into starters and rituals):**
- **Monday morning:** "Draft my weekly to-do triage" (Andrew) / "Draft this week's parent email" (Saska)
- **Tuesday camp prep email:** Saska auto-trigger
- **Wednesday league logistics:** Allison auto-trigger
- **Friday parent recap:** Saska auto-trigger
- **Friday 11am Compound Review:** All three

**The 30-day signal of true adoption:** A coach DMs Andrew their own GPT screenshot saying *"look what it just did"* without being asked. That is the moment you have a real team tool.

## Per-coach activation notes

### Andrew (Founder GPT)

- **Adoption risk:** Low. He requested this. He's already wired for it.
- **Watch out for:** Over-reliance. If he stops drafting from scratch entirely, voice could drift. Mitigation: one "no GPT" email per day on something high-stakes. Stay sharp.
- **Sweet-spot use:** Inbox triage in the morning, strategic decision support in the afternoon, weekly pattern surfacing on Friday.

### Allison (Adult Coach GPT)

- **Adoption risk:** Medium. She's organized but may default to "I'll just write it myself, it's faster."
- **Watch out for:** First-week silence. If she doesn't use it Day 2 or 3, Andrew should physically sit with her and run one parent email together.
- **Sweet-spot use:** USTA league logistics (the GPT genuinely knows more than she has time to memorize), weekly captain updates, scheduling/makeup decisions.
- **The hook:** *"Every USTA rule lookup that used to take 10 minutes now takes 30 seconds."* Lead with that.

### Saska (Junior Coach GPT)

- **Adoption risk:** Medium-high. She's on courts most of the day with kids. Email isn't her primary verb.
- **Watch out for:** Voice fit. Parents of young kids are emotionally sensitive. The GPT must sound *more like Saska* than the founder. Tune voice in the first 2 weeks aggressively.
- **Sweet-spot use:** Weekly progress summaries (high volume, repetitive, GPT crushes this), camp prep emails (also templatable), pathway recommendations for parents asking "what's next?"
- **The hook:** *"Friday parent emails go from 90 minutes to 15."* That's the win that creates the habit.

### Front Desk GPT

- **Adoption risk:** N/A — public-facing.
- **Watch out for:** Public-launch readiness. Don't embed until the soft-launch criteria in `post-dogfood-compound-loop.md` are all ✅.
- **Sweet-spot use:** First-touch FAQ answering, trial booking nudges, basic founder story for prospects. Never answers anything personal.

## The 5 starter questions every coach should try in their first session

These are designed to produce a "wow" within 5 minutes:

1. **"Here's a parent email. Draft a reply in my voice."** (Real input, real output, instant value.)
2. **"What's the difference between Seasonal and Committed pricing?"** (Tests grounding. The GPT should pull exact prices.)
3. **"A parent wants a refund after 45 days. What's our actual policy?"** (Tests the 30-day guarantee + account credit logic.)
4. **"Can you give me the founder story in 3 sentences for a prospect email?"** (Tests voice + brevity.)
5. **"My player has been crying after sessions. What should I do?"** (Tests the kill switch — should route to Andrew, not draft a customer reply.)

If the GPT nails 4 of 5, the coach is sold. If it misses 2+, that's the first compound cycle's job.

## Failure modes (and how to catch them early)

| Symptom | What it means | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Coach uses it Day 1, never Day 3 | First answer was off-voice or wrong | 1:1 sit-down. Identify the moment it lost trust. Fix that pattern. |
| Coach copies replies but always edits 4–5 lines | Voice is generic, not theirs | Add 5 of their actual past replies to their voice file in `gpt/03-KNOWLEDGE-BRAIN/voice-samples/` (e.g. `allison-voice.md`). Tell them to ask the GPT to "match the voice in the samples." |
| Coach asks it questions and gets "I don't know" 3+ times | Knowledge gap | Log the questions. Andrew updates the relevant knowledge file by Friday. |
| Coach starts using it for things outside its lane | Excitement bleed — they're treating it like a magic 8-ball | Re-anchor: "It's a coach's assistant for [specific scope]. For [other thing], use [other tool/person]." |
| Coach stops logging in the dogfood log | Friction or shame about misses | Move to async Slack-thread logging. Lower the bar. The log is for *learning*, not *grading*. |

## Closing

You can build the smartest GPT in the world and it won't take weight off your shoulders if Allison and Saska don't use it daily. The activation playbook is the bridge between "we have it" and "we use it."

Do the 20-minute first session in person. Do the 7-day ramp with daily check-ins. Do the Friday Compound Review every Friday for 12 weeks straight.

By Day 90, the GPT is a quiet third coach who knows the academy. By Year 1, it has read every parent thread you've ever written and sounds like you on a great day.

That is what 10/10 looks like.
