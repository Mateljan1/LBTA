# LBTA Team Rituals — How the GPTs Get Sharper Every Week

> Without a ritual, the GPTs decay. With one, they compound. Total time per week: 30 minutes.

---

## The core ritual: Friday Compound Review

**When:** Every Friday, 4:30pm – 5:00pm Pacific (or equivalent — the point is *every* week).

**Who:** Andrew + Allison + Saska. 30 minutes. Standing. Or 15 minutes async via Slack thread when in-person isn't possible.

**Why:** The GPTs are only as good as the knowledge files they're grounded in. The Friday ritual is how the team's collective experience gets baked into the shared brain.

---

## The 30-minute agenda


| Minute | Step                                                                                                                                                                                                        | Who           |
| ------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------- |
| 0–5    | Each person shares **1 great answer** their GPT gave this week (something they'd send as-is to a customer). Read it out loud.                                                                               | Round-robin   |
| 5–15   | Each person shares **1 weak answer** their GPT gave this week (where they had to rewrite, where it got a fact wrong, or where it sounded off-voice). What did the rewrite look like?                        | Round-robin   |
| 15–25  | Andrew picks **1–3 fixes** from the weak answers that go into the knowledge files this week. Examples: a new FAQ entry, a price update, a clarified policy, a removed forbidden word, a new email template. | Andrew + team |
| 25–30  | Andrew (or whoever has Cursor/repo access) updates the knowledge file(s), re-uploads to all six GPTs, and posts in Slack: "Updated this week: [list]."                                                      | Andrew        |


**Key rule:** No more than 3 knowledge-file changes per week. More than that = the GPTs feel different every week and trust erodes. Less than 1 = the team isn't learning. The sweet spot is 1–3.

## Weekly metrics (tracked by Andrew, reported in the Friday ritual)

The 10/10 test, measured weekly:


| Metric                                                      | Owner          | Target by week 4    | Target by week 8 |
| ----------------------------------------------------------- | -------------- | ------------------- | ---------------- |
| Hours saved on inbox + admin                                | Andrew         | 5+ hrs/week         | 8+ hrs/week      |
| Hours saved on league + scheduling                          | Allison        | 3+ hrs/week         | 5+ hrs/week      |

| # of knowledge-file edits made (lower is good after week 4) | Andrew         | <8 cumulative edits | <3 edits/month   |


If we're not hitting these by week 8, the architecture is wrong, not the GPTs. We re-design.

## Logging great + weak answers (do this all week, not just on Friday)

Each team member keeps a running list during the week — not from memory at 4:30pm Friday.

**Easy method:** at the bottom of each day's GPT chat, paste a one-liner into a shared Notion page or Slack thread:

```
Great: "GPT drafted parent reply about camp packing — sent as-is, parent thanked me"
Weak: "GPT got the camp pickup time wrong (said 12pm, actual is 12:30pm) — knowledge file fix needed"
```

By Friday you have 5–10 logs each. The ritual becomes pulling 1 great + 1 weak from the list, not racking your brain.

## Quarterly: the deeper compound

Every 3 months (Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4), block 90 minutes for a deeper review:

- **What's the GPT *not* doing yet that it should be?** (new connections, new starters, new role-specific GPT?)
- **Are the metrics moving?** (compare to baseline — see "Weekly metrics" table)
- **Has anything changed about the academy** that the knowledge files don't reflect? (new coach, new program, new pricing, new site, new partnership)
- **Should we expand to a new GPT?** (e.g. a "Camp Director GPT" for summer, a "Tournament Travel GPT" for HP players)

Document each quarterly review as `gpt/quarterly-reviews/YYYY-Qn.md` so the next review can build on it.

## What "10/10" actually means here

A 10/10 doesn't mean the GPT is perfect on day 1. It means:

1. **The architecture matches the team's real jobs** (6 GPTs, not 1)
2. **There's a measurable weight-off-Andrew outcome** (5+ hours/week saved by week 4)
3. **There's a learning loop** (Friday ritual, weekly knowledge file updates, logged metrics)
4. **It's safe to scale** (Front Desk GPT can go public without risk because it's locked to published facts only)
5. **It compounds** (week 12 is meaningfully better than week 1, because the team has fed the brain 30+ improvements by then)

If these five are true, you're at 10/10. If any one is missing, you're not — and we know exactly which lever to pull.
