# LBTA Founder GPT — Activation Script (Andrew, Day 1)

> 20 minutes. One sitting. Goal: turn this from "a saved GPT" into "a habit Andrew uses three times before lunch tomorrow."

---

## Why a script

Custom GPTs die when nobody opens them. The first 20 minutes are the difference between "another tab I forget" and "the most useful tool on my laptop." This script forces three shipped outcomes on day 1.

## Prep (2 min)

- Quiet room. Phone face-down.
- Two browser tabs: the GPT, and Gmail.
- One real parent email Andrew has been putting off — open in front of him.
- Today's calendar in view.

## Round 1 — Ship one draft (8 min)

**Prompt the GPT (paste verbatim):**

> *"I'll paste a parent email below. Draft a reply in my voice — calm, founder, specific. Stay under 8 sentences. End with `support@lagunabeachtennisacademy.com` and `(949) 534-0457`. If anything in the email needs Andrew's personal call, flag it — don't decide for me."*
>
> *"[paste email]"*

**Andrew's job:**
- Read the draft once. Don't edit yet.
- Ask: would Andrew sign this? If yes — copy to Gmail, edit one line if it needs it, **send.**
- If no — tell the GPT what's off ("too formal," "missed the empathy beat," "wrong policy") and ask for a v2.

**Outcome:** one parent email shipped. Time saved counted.

## Round 2 — Pre-mortem one decision (6 min)

**Andrew names a decision he's been chewing on.** Examples:
- "Should I take Allison to a 4th adult clinic per week or hire a sub?"
- "Should I push the camp price to $525 next year?"
- "Should I bring on Coach X part-time?"

**Prompt the GPT:**

> *"Pre-mortem this decision. Imagine it's six months from now and the call I'm about to make turned out to be wrong. Walk me through the three most likely failure modes, ranked by how badly they'd hurt LBTA. Then give me the single question I should answer before deciding. Stay under 200 words. Don't decide for me."*

**Andrew's job:**
- Read once. Sit with it for 60 seconds.
- Notice which failure mode tightens his stomach. That's the real risk.
- Write the question down. Sleep on it.

**Outcome:** one decision moved from gut to structured. Confidence up or down (both are wins).

## Round 3 — Curve check (4 min)

**Prompt the GPT:**

> *"Curve check on three things on my plate this week: [paste 3 tasks]. For each, label Curve 1 (capped payoff — satisfice and ship) or Curve 2 (uncapped payoff — sharpen my thinking). Then give me ONE sentence on what to do with each."*

**Andrew's job:**
- Read once.
- For Curve 1 items — finish them today, fast. 85% is fine.
- For Curve 2 items — block 90 minutes this week, no laptop, just a notebook.

**Outcome:** the week reshapes itself. Less reactive. More leverage.

---

## Closing (under 1 min)

Pin the GPT to the favorites bar in Chrome. Add to phone home screen. Pick a habit trigger for tomorrow:

> "When a parent email lands → open Founder GPT first, draft second, send third."

If by Friday Andrew has used the GPT for parent comms 5+ times, the day-1 script worked.

## What success looks like 7 days from now

- Time saved on parent comms: ≥ 30 min/day
- Decisions pre-mortemed: ≥ 2 in the week
- Curve checks done: at least Monday and Wednesday
- One time the GPT was *wrong* and Andrew caught it (this is healthy — log to `05-OPERATIONS/eval/dogfood-log-template.md`)

## What failure looks like

- "I forgot to open it for 3 days." → Trigger isn't tight enough. Try: "Every parent email gets pasted into the GPT before Andrew replies, no exceptions, for 7 days."
- "It writes weird, not like me." → Drift report. Open `05-OPERATIONS/runbooks/voice-drift.md`.
- "It made up a price." → Hallucination report. Open `05-OPERATIONS/runbooks/hallucination-incident.md`.

---

**Andrew, when in doubt:** ship the draft, count the minutes, log the wins. The GPT compounds with use, not with theorizing.
