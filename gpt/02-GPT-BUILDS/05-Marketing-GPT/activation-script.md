# LBTA Marketing GPT — Activation Script (Andrew, Day 1)

> 30 minutes. One sitting. Goal: turn this from "a saved GPT" into "the tool I open every Sunday at 7pm to write the newsletter."

---

## Why a script

Marketing GPTs die two ways: (1) Andrew forgets to open it, or (2) Andrew uses it once and the draft sounds generic. This script forces three shippable outputs on day 1 — one IG carousel, one partner email, one newsletter section — so the GPT proves itself before it gets a chance to become forgettable.

## Prep (3 min)

- Quiet room. Phone face-down.
- Two browser tabs: the GPT, and the live site (`lagunabeachtennisacademy.com`).
- One real partner Andrew has been meaning to email — name + venue + why-them — written in a sticky note.
- One thing from this week's coaching that's blog-worthy (a moment, an insight, a parent comment). One sentence is enough.
- The Sunday-night newsletter slot in the calendar — confirm it's still on.

## Round 1 — Ship one IG carousel (10 min)

**Pick the topic in 30 seconds.** One coaching idea from this week. Examples:
- "Why we don't run drills before warmup."
- "The drop shot most juniors never get taught."
- "Three things parents misunderstand about footwork."

**Prompt the GPT (paste verbatim, fill the bracket):**

> *"Draft an Instagram carousel on this idea: `[paste idea]`. 6 slides. Slide 1 hook (one sensory line, no exclamation). Slides 2–5 develop the idea, one beat per slide. Slide 6 soft CTA (book a trial OR come watch a clinic — pick the one that fits). Caption underneath: 80–120 words, in my voice, 3–5 hashtags, no forbidden words. Then list the 6 slide texts cleanly so I can paste them into Canva."*

**Andrew's job:**
- Read once. Don't edit yet.
- Ask: would Andrew post this without changing the voice? If yes — copy slide texts into Canva, build the carousel, **post it Monday morning.**
- If no — tell the GPT what's off ("slide 3 sounds like a coach, not a founder," "the hook is too generic") and ask for v2.

**Outcome:** one IG carousel ready for Monday morning. The GPT has paid for itself by 7:35pm.

## Round 2 — Draft one partner email (8 min)

**Andrew names the partner.** Examples:
- "The owner of the Surf & Sand racquet club."
- "The PE director at Top of the World Elementary."
- "A local optometrist who could co-host a vision-and-tennis clinic."

**Prompt the GPT (paste verbatim, fill the brackets):**

> *"Draft a partner outreach email to `[name + venue]`. Why them, why now: `[1 sentence]`. Goal of the partnership: `[1 sentence]`. Write it founder-direct, calm, under 250 words. Specific opening line (not 'Hope you're well!'), three short paragraphs, soft CTA (a 15-min call OR a court visit — your pick). Sign-off block: name = Andrew Mateljan, title = Founder & Director of Tennis, contact = (949) 534-0457 / support@lagunabeachtennisacademy.com. Flag anything I should personally fact-check before sending."*

**Andrew's job:**
- Read once. Sit with it for 30 seconds.
- Ask: does the opening line sound like Andrew opened it on a Tuesday morning, not a copywriter polished it on a Friday? If yes — copy to Gmail, **send.**
- If no — say what's off and ask for v2.

**Outcome:** one partner email shipped. The single highest-leverage marketing surface, done in 8 minutes.

## Round 3 — Draft one newsletter section (8 min)

**Pick the section in 30 seconds.** It's Sunday — what's the headline this week? Examples:
- A program callout (camp registration opening).
- A philosophy beat (one paragraph from Andrew's notes).
- A community moment (a player win, a coach observation, a returning family).

**Prompt the GPT (paste verbatim, fill the bracket):**

> *"Draft the founder-note section of this week's Sunday newsletter on this topic: `[paste topic]`. ~200 words, my voice, anchor line allowed (one max). End with one specific next step, not a CTA in marketing language. Then give me three eyebrow + headline pairs — calm, specific, no exclamation, no clickbait — so I can pick the one that fits."*

**Andrew's job:**
- Read once.
- Pick the eyebrow/headline pair that sounds most like Sunday-night Andrew.
- Paste the founder-note into ActiveCampaign's draft. Andrew finishes the rest of the newsletter and **schedules send for Sunday 7pm.**

**Outcome:** Sunday newsletter is 70% done by Day 1.

---

## Closing (under 1 min)

Pin the GPT to the favorites bar in Chrome. Add to phone home screen. Pick the Sunday trigger:

> "Sunday 7pm: open Marketing GPT first, draft newsletter second, schedule third."

If by next Sunday Andrew has used the GPT for IG, partner outreach, and newsletter all once each, the day-1 script worked.

## What success looks like 7 days from now

- IG carousels shipped: ≥ 1
- Partner emails sent: ≥ 1 (the single highest-leverage marketing action)
- Newsletter sent on time: 1
- Time saved on marketing comms: ≥ 90 min for the week
- One time the GPT was *wrong* and Andrew caught it before sending (this is healthy — log to `05-OPERATIONS/eval/dogfood-log-template.md`)

## What failure looks like

- "I forgot to open it for 3 days." → Trigger isn't tight enough. Try: "Sunday 7pm and Wednesday 10am are GPT-open windows. No exceptions for two weeks."
- "It writes generic, not like me." → Voice drift. Open `05-OPERATIONS/runbooks/voice-drift.md`. Re-upload `voice-samples/marketing-voice.md` with two more samples in Andrew's voice.
- "It used a forbidden word." → Open `05-OPERATIONS/runbooks/marketing-incident.md`. Don't ship that draft. Add the failed prompt to the red-team probe set.
- "The image gen output looked too real, I almost posted it." → That's the kill switch. The GPT is doing the right thing labeling it `DRAFT`. Tighten the rule: AI visuals never get exported, period.

---

**Andrew, when in doubt:** ship the draft, count the minutes, log the wins. The Marketing GPT compounds with use, not with theorizing — but it only compounds if every send still passes through Andrew's eyes.
