# Conversation Starters — LBTA Marketing GPT

> Habit triggers, not curiosity prompts. Each starter maps to a real weekly task that takes Andrew 1–3 hours when done from scratch.
>
> Paste these (max 4) into the Conversation Starters field of the Custom GPT Builder.

---

## The 4 starters (paste these)

1. **Draft this week's Sunday-night newsletter — eyebrow, headline, ~200-word founder note, three program callouts, soft CTA.**
2. **Give me an Instagram carousel (5–7 slides) on one idea from this week's coaching — slide 1 hook, slides 2–6 develop, slide 7 soft CTA.**
3. **Draft a partner outreach email to [name / venue / brand] — specific hook, 3-paragraph pitch, calm CTA, under 250 words.**
4. **Pick one program or moment from the live site and draft a 1,200–1,500 word blog post in my voice — no SEO sludge, no AI prose.**

---

## Why these four

- **#1 Newsletter** — the most important cadence beat. Misses Sunday → audience drift. This starter pays for the GPT in the first month.
- **#2 IG carousel** — highest reach-per-effort surface on Instagram. One starter → one shippable carousel.
- **#3 Partner outreach** — the surface Andrew procrastinates most on. The right partner email is worth more than 30 IG posts.
- **#4 Blog** — long-form is where the philosophy lives. SEO-relevant, shareable, evergreen — and the easiest place for AI prose to leak in. The starter forces the founder voice.

---

## Backup starters (rotate seasonally)

- *"Write three IG single-post variations on [topic / camp / clinic] — A is the calmest, B is the most specific, C is the most editorial."*
- *"Audit my last 8 IG captions — flag forbidden words, voice drift, generic AI prose. Rewrite the worst three."*
- *"Draft a 5-email enrollment sequence for the [Spring / Summer / Fall] season — calm, founder voice, soft CTAs."*
- *"Take this engagement CSV and tell me the one post type I should run more of next month."*

Swap in when seasonal needs change (camp launch, year-end retros, league registration windows, partner pushes).

---

## Voice check rule (for the Marketing GPT)

Before any draft, the GPT runs the **5-pass review** silently:

1. **Rough** — generate the draft for the surface.
2. **Voice** — does it sound like the website and voice samples? Rewrite if generic.
3. **Brand** — anchor line if appropriate, tone restrained, tokens consistent.
4. **Forbidden words** — none of: *elite, world-class, mastery, maximize, boost, unlock, level up, game-changer, transformational, unleash, dominate, crush it, sign up now, don't miss out, limited spots*.
5. **Factuality** — every concrete claim citable to knowledge brain or live site, or flagged `[NEEDS ANDREW VERIFY]`.

If two passes fail, the GPT returns the best draft with a one-line note about what failed. No silent shipping of slop.

---

## When to swap a starter

If a starter isn't getting used after 2 weeks, swap it for one Andrew (or Phase 2 operator) is actually copy-pasting into the GPT every week. The starter slot is precious — make every one of the four a habit, not a curiosity.
