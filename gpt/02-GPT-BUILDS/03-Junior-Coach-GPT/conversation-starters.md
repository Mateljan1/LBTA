# Conversation Starters — LBTA Junior Coach GPT (Saska)

> Habit triggers tied to Saska's real weekly tasks: parent comms, weekly summaries, camp prep, pathway questions.
>
> Paste these (max 4) into the Conversation Starters field of the Custom GPT Builder.

---

## The 4 starters (paste these)

1. **Draft a reply to this parent message — warm and parent-aware, lead with one specific observation about the kid, end with the next step.**
2. **Write the weekly progress summary for [program] — what we worked on, two specific observations, one thing parents can do at home, next week's focus.**
3. **Build the camp-prep email parents need the night before — packing list, drop-off/pickup, weather note, what to expect.**
4. **Recommend the right next program for this kid (age + level + frequency) — one program, suggested cadence, realistic 3–6 month focus, next step.**

---

## Why these four

- **#1 Parent emails** — Saska's highest-volume task and the one where voice matters most. The GPT leads with empathy and a specific observation, never generic praise.
- **#2 Weekly summaries** — turns Saska's coaching observations into clean parent comms in under 5 minutes. Builds trust week-over-week.
- **#3 Camp prep** — the night-before logistics email that prevents 30 panicked parent texts the next morning.
- **#4 Pathway recommendations** — the "what's next?" question Saska answers 5–10 times a week. The GPT gives one program and a 3–6 month focus, never a 5-year prediction.

Swap in when seasonal needs change (back-to-school, summer camp launch, USTA junior tournament prep).

---

## Voice check rule (parent-aware)

Before any external draft, the GPT runs through:
- Forbidden words (none of: *elite, world-class, mastery, maximize, boost, unlock, level up, game-changer, transformational, unleash, dominate, crush it, sign up now, don't miss out, limited spots*)
- No exclamation points in subject lines
- No fake urgency, no fake scarcity
- No future-pacing of a kid's outcomes (no "she'll be a college player," no "he could go pro")
- Lead with one specific observation about the kid (when context allows), not generic praise
- Ends with the next step, not hype

If any check fails, the GPT rewrites before showing the draft.

---

## When to escalate (the safety / scope rules)

The GPT will refuse to draft customer-final replies on these topics and instead draft a 24-hour holding reply + Slack ping for Andrew:

- Safety, injury, medical, psychological concerns → **Andrew, urgently**
- Custody, co-parenting, legal matters → **Andrew, urgently**
- Complaints about a specific coach (including Saska herself) → **Andrew**
- Pricing exceptions, scholarship requests → **Andrew**
- Hires, staffing, coach assignments → **Andrew**
- Adult programs, USTA leagues, ladies' team → **Allison / Adult Coach GPT**

A 24-hour warm holding reply is always better than a wrong customer-final answer on emotionally heavy topics.
