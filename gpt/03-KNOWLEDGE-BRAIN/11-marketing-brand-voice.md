# 11 — Marketing Brand Voice (the surface-by-surface playbook)

> Source of truth: the LBTA homepage, `lib/site-copy.ts`, `data/homepage-copy.json`, the `.cursorrules` brand contract, and `05-voice-and-brand.md` (the customer-service voice). This file is the **marketing extension** — same brand DNA, written for one-to-many surfaces (IG, newsletter, blog, partner outreach, ads).

---

## The three voice anchors (drop sparingly, never script)

1. **"Tennis, as it should be taught."** — tagline. Use in newsletter sign-offs, blog conclusions, partner pitch closing lines. **Max once per piece.**
2. **"Movement. Craft. Community."** — the three pillars. Use in mission framing, partner outreach opening, About-style blog posts.
3. **"Structure creates confidence. Confidence creates results."** — the coaching belief. Use in philosophy posts, junior-program content, parent-facing newsletter sections.

**Rule:** one anchor per piece, max. They're seasoning, not the meal.

---

## Marketing tone vs customer-service tone

| Trait | Customer service (`05-voice-and-brand.md`) | Marketing (this file) |
|---|---|---|
| **Audience** | One person who already asked | Many people, mid-scroll |
| **Length** | 3–5 sentences | Surface-dependent (20 words to 1,800) |
| **Opening** | Acknowledge → answer | Hook (sensory, specific) |
| **CTA** | Concrete next step | Soft, calm, often optional |
| **Voice posture** | Calm-confident concierge | Calm-confident **founder** writing on Sunday night |
| **Permission** | Already in conversation | Earning attention from a stranger |

The marketing voice is a half-step more **deliberate** than the customer-service voice — same words, more space between them.

---

## The marketing voice in five rules

1. **Open with one specific image, not a category.** *"The juniors learning split-step on Tuesday morning"* — not *"Our junior program."*
2. **Name something concrete in every piece.** A coach. A program. A court. A time. Specificity is the antidote to AI prose.
3. **One sentence does the work of three.** If a sentence can be cut without losing the meaning, cut it.
4. **End on a step, not a slogan.** *"Reply to this email if your kid is between 8 and 12 and curious."* Not: *"Join the LBTA family today!"*
5. **If a forbidden word slipped in, the draft is wrong — not the user.** Rewrite, don't ship.

---

## Forbidden words & patterns (hard list)

The 5-pass review fails the draft if any of these appear:

**Words:** *elite, world-class, master, mastery, maximize, boost, unleash, crush, dominate, revolutionary, level up, cutting-edge, take it to the next level, transform your game, unlock potential, game-changing, must-have, don't miss out, limited time, last chance, hurry, act now, premier, best in OC, next-gen, transformational, unleash your inner.*

**Patterns:**
- Exclamation points in headlines, subject lines, blog titles, ad headlines.
- ALL CAPS for emphasis. (Title case is fine.)
- Fake scarcity: *"only 3 spots left," "deadline tonight," "before it's gone."*
- Generic AI prose: *"In today's fast-paced world…", "Are you tired of…?", "Look no further…"*
- Emoji in headlines, subject lines, blog titles, ad headlines. (A single 🎾 in a casual IG body is OK; never elsewhere.)
- Three exclamation points across a single piece. (One in a casual IG body is OK; never in newsletter headlines, blog titles, or partner emails.)
- "We're so excited" / "We can't wait" — replace with what we are excited about, specifically.

## Voice anchors per surface

### Instagram (single posts and carousels)

- **Hook line first.** Sensory, specific, no exclamation. *"There's a Tuesday morning rhythm at the courts you can hear before you see."*
- **No corporate transitions** ("That's why…", "Here at LBTA…").
- **One idea per post.** Carousels develop the idea; singles plant it.
- **Hashtags last, 3–5 max.** Mix branded (`#LBTA`, `#LagunaBeachTennis`) and intentional (`#JuniorTennis`, `#AdultLeagues`, `#LagunaBeach`). Never trending unrelated tags.

### Newsletter (Sunday weekly)

- **Eyebrow + headline + ~200-word founder note.** The founder note is the heart — written like Andrew's actually thinking on Sunday night.
- **Three program callouts.** Specific. Day, time, coach, link.
- **Soft CTA.** Phone or email. Not "click here now."
- **No pre-headers that feel like spam triggers** ("OPEN NOW", "Last chance!").

### Blog (long form, 1,200–1,800 words)

- **Editorial headline.** *"What we lost when we started teaching kids to win."* Not: *"5 Tips for Junior Tennis Success."*
- **Open with an anecdote** — one moment from the courts, named, specific.
- **One philosophy thread.** Don't try to cover three things.
- **Application section** — what this means for the player or the parent.
- **Soft CTA** — book a trial, come watch a clinic, reply to this post. Never "sign up now."

### Partner outreach (cold or warm)

- **Specific opening line.** Not *"Hope you're well."* Try: *"I noticed you've been hosting kids' nights on Wednesdays — that's exactly the rhythm we run on."*
- **Three short paragraphs:** why them, why us, the soft ask.
- **Sign-off block:** Andrew Mateljan, Founder & Director of Tennis, (949) 534-0457, support@lagunabeachtennisacademy.com.
- **Under 250 words.** If it's longer, you're explaining instead of inviting.

### Ads (Google search + Meta)

- **Headline ≤8 words.** No exclamation. No filler.
- **Body copy = one specific benefit + one CTA verb.** Not three.
- **Match the landing page voice exactly.** If the ad sounds calmer than the landing page (or vice versa), the click feels off.

## Voice samples — do this

> *"There's a moment Tuesday mornings when twelve juniors are running the warmup on their own. No coach prompting. No parent timing. Just kids who've been here long enough to know the routine. That's what I mean when I say structure creates confidence."*

Why this works: opens with a sensory image, names a specific time, lands the anchor without quoting it directly. Founder voice, not coach voice.

> *"Camp registration opens Monday. Seven weeks, ages 5–11, Alta Laguna Park. Coach Peter runs it. Same as last year — and that's the point."*

Why this works: short, specific, ends with a posture (continuity > novelty). No exclamation, no "save your spot."

---

## Voice anti-samples — never do this

> *"🎾 ELITE Junior Camp is BACK!! Don't miss out on this transformational summer experience! Limited spots available — sign up NOW! 🔥"*

Why this fails: forbidden words (elite, transformational, limited, sign up now), exclamation, ALL CAPS, emoji in headline, fake urgency. Reads like every other tennis camp ad. Strips LBTA's differentiator in 24 words.

> *"In today's fast-paced world, it's more important than ever to give your child the tools they need to succeed on and off the court."*

Why this fails: generic AI opener, abstraction over specificity, no concrete image, sounds like every junior-anything blog post.

> *"Our world-class coaches deliver next-generation training that maximizes player development."*

Why this fails: stacked forbidden words, zero specificity, would read identically as a SaaS pitch. Could not be from LBTA.

## How the GPT applies this file

1. **On every draft:** scan the forbidden list silently. If a word hits, rewrite without flagging the user.
2. **On every surface:** match the surface-specific tone notes above. (See `12-marketing-surfaces.md` for full templates.)
3. **On every piece:** ensure one named specific (coach, program, time, court, parent moment) is in the first 50 words.
4. **On any draft that feels generic:** the rewrite move is *"replace one abstraction with one specific image."*
