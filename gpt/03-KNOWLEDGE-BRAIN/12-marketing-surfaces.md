# 12 — Marketing Surfaces (templates + worked examples)

> Every marketing surface LBTA uses, with the exact shape, length, structure, and a worked example. The GPT references this file on every draft.
>
> Source: `lib/site-copy.ts`, homepage hero copy, the active newsletter cadence (Sunday 7pm via ActiveCampaign), Andrew's IG presence, and the partner outreach pattern documented in `gpt/01-ARCHITECTURE/team-architecture.md`.

## Surfaces at a glance

| Surface | Cadence | Length | Depth | Internal review |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Instagram carousel** | 1–2 / week | 5–7 slides + caption | Deep | Full 5-pass |
| **Instagram single post** | 2–3 / week | Image + 80–150 word caption | Light | Full 5-pass |
| **Instagram story** | 2–4 / week | 2–4 frames | Light | Voice + forbidden-words pass |
| **Newsletter (weekly)** | Sunday 7pm | Eyebrow + headline + ~200w founder note + 3 callouts | Deep | Full 5-pass |
| **Blog post** | 2 / month | 1,200–1,800 words | Deep | Full 5-pass + factuality |
| **Partner outreach email** | As needed | ≤250 words | Deep | Full 5-pass + factuality |
| **Google Search ad** | Quarterly refresh | Headline ≤8 words + 90-char body | Light | Voice + forbidden-words pass |
| **Meta (FB/IG) ad** | Quarterly refresh | Headline ≤8 words + 125-char body + visual brief | Light | Voice + forbidden-words pass |
| **Enrollment email sequence** | Seasonal (5 emails) | 150–250 words each | Deep | Full 5-pass |

**Deep** surfaces get the full 5-pass review (rough → voice → brand → forbidden words → factuality). **Light** surfaces get a 2-pass minimum (voice + forbidden words). Andrew always reviews before send/post regardless.

## 1. Instagram carousel

### Shape

- **Slide 1:** hook — one sensory line, no exclamation, no question that sounds like clickbait. Subhead optional, ≤6 words.
- **Slides 2–6:** one beat per slide. Each slide has one idea. Header line + 1–2 sentences max.
- **Slide 7 (or final):** soft CTA. *"Book a trial. (949) 534-0457."* Or: *"Reply if your kid is between 8 and 12."* Never *"DM us NOW."*
- **Caption underneath:** 80–150 words, founder voice, expands on the carousel arc, ends with one CTA. 3–5 hashtags max.

### Worked example

**Topic:** "Why we don't run drills before warmup."

- **Slide 1:** *Warmup is the lesson.* (Subhead: *Three reasons we changed this.*)
- **Slide 2:** *The body learns rhythm before it learns shots.* / If a kid can't sequence a side-step → split-step → recovery, no amount of drilling fixes the rally.
- **Slide 3:** *Five minutes of footwork beats fifteen minutes of forehands.* / We run movement for the first five — every session, every age.
- **Slide 4:** *Confidence shows up in the shoes, not the racquet.* / Watch a junior who's warm. Watch one who isn't. The difference is visible before the first ball.
- **Slide 5:** *Structure creates confidence.* / This is the rhythm of every LBTA session — junior or adult, beginner or league.
- **Slide 6:** *Come watch a clinic.* (Soft CTA — Tuesdays at 3:30, Moulton Meadows. Reply or call (949) 534-0457.)

**Caption:** *Most junior tennis programs spend the first ten minutes feeding forehands. We don't. The body learns rhythm before it learns shots — and a kid who's warmed up is a kid who's coachable for the next 50 minutes. This is one of the small things that compounds across a season. If your kid is between 8 and 12 and you've been thinking about a more structured group, Tuesday afternoons are open. Reply here or call (949) 534-0457.* `#LBTA #JuniorTennis #LagunaBeach #TennisCoaching`

## 2. Instagram single post

### Shape

- **Image** (real, on-court, consent-cleared per `13-marketing-asset-library.md`).
- **First line of caption = hook.** No "Today we…" / "We're excited to…"
- **Body:** 60–120 words, one beat.
- **CTA line:** soft, one option.
- **Hashtags:** 3–5, last.

### Worked example

*The Tuesday morning before camp opens — empty courts, taped lines, twelve folding chairs in a row. We line them up the day before because the first ten minutes of camp is the hardest moment of the week. If kids walk in and the court already looks like it's expecting them, the day starts in a different posture. Camp opens Monday. Ages 5–11, Alta Laguna Park, Coach Peter. (949) 534-0457 if your kid is in.* `#LBTA #SummerCamp #LagunaBeach`

## 3. Instagram story

### Shape

- **2–4 frames.** Each frame = one image + ≤8 words on screen.
- **Use cases:** behind-the-scenes (coach prepping court), reposts (parent sharing a player win — with permission), a moment from a clinic, a calm calendar nudge ("Camp opens Monday" + link sticker).
- **No countdown stickers,** no GIFs that don't fit the LBTA aesthetic.

### Templates

- *Behind-the-scenes:* "Tuesday morning. Court 3. The juniors warming up." → "This is the part most programs skip."
- *Calendar nudge:* "Camp registration opens Monday." → "Ages 5–11. Alta Laguna Park."
- *Repost:* (parent's photo, with permission) + "From a parent. Tuesday clinic. Thank you."

## 5. Blog post (1,200–1,800 words)

### Shape

- **Editorial headline.** No "5 Tips for…" / "How to…"
- **Sub-headline.** ≤14 words, completes the headline's promise.
- **Open with an anecdote.** One named specific scene from the courts (use the Asset Library — never invent player names).
- **One philosophy thread, three sections.**
- **Application section.** What this means for the player or parent.
- **Soft CTA.** Book a trial, come watch a clinic, reply.
- **Word count:** 1,200–1,800. Lower fails to develop the idea; higher loses the reader.

### Title patterns that work

- *"What we lost when we started teaching kids to win."*
- *"The drop shot most juniors are never taught."*
- *"Three things parents misunderstand about footwork."*
- *"Why our six-week beginner clinic isn't called a beginner clinic."*

### Title patterns to avoid

- *"5 Tips for Junior Tennis Success."*
- *"The Ultimate Guide to Tennis in Laguna Beach."*
- *"How to Become a Better Tennis Player Today!"*

### Worked opening (first 80 words)

*The first time a parent told me their kid "just needed more reps," I was 28 and didn't know how to disagree without sounding like I was selling something. Twenty-five years later I disagree all the time. Reps without rhythm isn't practice — it's running into the wall. The juniors who develop fastest at LBTA aren't the ones who hit the most balls. They're the ones who learn to move first. Here's why we built the program around that.*

## 6. Partner outreach email

### Shape

- **Subject line:** specific, ≤8 words. *"Tuesday clinics + Surf & Sand?"* — not *"Partnership Opportunity."*
- **Opening line:** specific to them, observation-based. Not *"Hope you're doing well!"*
- **Three short paragraphs:** why them, why us, the soft ask.
- **Soft CTA:** *"Worth a 15-min call this week?"* or *"Open to a court visit?"*
- **Sign-off block:** Andrew Mateljan, Founder & Director of Tennis, (949) 534-0457, support@lagunabeachtennisacademy.com.
- **Under 250 words total.**

> **Subject:** Tuesday clinics + Surf & Sand?
>

We'd handle scheduling and instruction; you'd own the member relationship and the venue. Smaller than a full programming partnership — more like a pilot we could run for six weeks and see what happens.

> Open to a 15-min call this week, or a court visit on Tuesday at 3:30 if you'd rather see how we run a session before committing to anything?
>
> — Andrew

## 7. Google Search ad

### Shape

- **Headlines (3 × 30 chars):** specific, no exclamation, no "Best/Top/Premier."
- **Description (90 chars):** one specific benefit + one calm CTA.
- **Display URL path:** `/junior-program` or `/adult-beginner` (matches landing page).
- **Landing page voice must match.** If the ad is calmer than the page, the click feels wrong.

### Worked example

- Headline 1: `Junior Tennis · Laguna Beach`
- Headline 2: `Movement-First Coaching · Ages 5–14`
- Headline 3: `Book a Trial Lesson Today`
- Description: `Structured group programs with founder-led coaches. Trial available — call (949) 534-0457.`
- URL path: `/junior-program`

## 8. Meta (FB/IG) ad

### Shape

- **Visual:** real photo, on-court, consent-cleared. Never AI-generated. Never stock.
- **Headline (≤8 words):** *"Junior tennis, the right way."*
- **Body (≤125 chars):** one specific benefit + one CTA verb.
- **CTA button:** "Learn More" or "Contact Us." Never "Sign Up."
- **Landing page must match.** Same headline, same voice.

### Worked example

- Visual: Tuesday-morning warmup line, ages 8–12, Moulton Meadows.
- Headline: `Junior tennis, taught for the long arc.`
- Body: `Movement-first coaching for ages 5–14 in Laguna Beach. Trial sessions available.`
- CTA: `Learn More` → `/junior-program`

## 9. Enrollment email sequence (seasonal, 5 emails)

### Shape

A 5-email sequence run via ActiveCampaign when a new season opens (Spring, Summer, Fall). Sent over 3 weeks.

| # | Day | Subject (≤8 words) | Length | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Day 0 | "Spring registration is open." | ~150w | Announce + link |
| 2 | Day 4 | "What changed this season." | ~200w | Specific updates (new coach, new clinic, new schedule) |
| 3 | Day 9 | "A note on how we run camp." | ~250w | Philosophy beat — why we do it this way |
| 4 | Day 14 | "Two weeks in — what's filling fast." | ~150w | Calm signal (not fake scarcity) |
| 5 | Day 20 | "Last call for spring sessions." | ~150w | Final, calm CTA |

### Voice rules for the sequence

- **No fake urgency.** "Filling fast" is OK if it's true. "Last 3 spots!" is forbidden unless the GPT can cite the exact roster.
- **Each email must work standalone.** Someone who only opens #3 should still get value.
- **Same voice across all 5.** Don't shift to "marketing voice" in #4 and #5 just because they're closer to the deadline.

When asked to draft any surface:

1. **Find the surface in this file.** Match the shape exactly.
2. **Apply `11-marketing-brand-voice.md`.** Voice rules and forbidden words.
