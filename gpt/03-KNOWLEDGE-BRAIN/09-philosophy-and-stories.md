## The founder

**Andrew Mateljan** is the founder and Director of Tennis at LBTA. 25 years coaching. GPTCA-ATP certified. Background spans developmental tennis, NCAA Division I placement, and ATP/WTA Tour coaching. Currently coaches **Karue Sell, ATP #262**. Previous players include **Max McKennon (ATP #458)** and **Ryan Seggerman (ATP career-high singles #72)**. Tour coaching includes Indian Wells Masters 1000.

Andrew also built **Fit4Tennis**, the online tennis fitness platform — 100,000+ users worldwide.

## The founding belief

Most tennis programs in the United States are extractive: they sell time on a court. LBTA is built on a different premise — **structure creates confidence, confidence creates results.**

That belief came from coaching at every level: developmental kids who'd never held a racquet, college players grinding for scholarships, and pros chasing ATP points. The pattern was the same. The players who made it weren't the most "talented" — they were the ones who'd been given a *system*. Movement first. Craft second. Community to hold both.

That's what LBTA is.

## Movement. Craft. Community.

Three pillars. They're not marketing. They're how every session is structured.

**Movement** is the foundation. Before we coach a forehand, we coach the player's body. Footwork, balance, athletic posture, recovery. If movement is broken, no amount of stroke instruction sticks. This is where Andrew's Fit4Tennis work plugs in directly.

**Craft** is the slow build of stroke quality, decision-making, and tactical understanding. Group sessions are designed in progressions — never random drills. Every session has a focus, a rep count, and a measurable outcome.

**Community** is the part most academies skip. Tennis is a lonely sport. We build classes where players know each other, parents know each other, and coaches know every family by name. We are intentionally small — three coaches, three lanes — so we can be that.

## The pathway

We coach the long arc, not the next session. From first swings (Little Tennis Stars at age 3–4) to college tennis (Junior Development → High Performance → NCAA placement). Every program connects.

A 7-year-old in Orange Ball today might be in High Performance at 14 and playing D1 at 18. We've placed 20+ players at NCAA Division I in the last 8 years. That's not because we recruit prodigies — it's because we coach the pathway.

## Why three locations

We chose three sites — Moulton Meadows, Alta Laguna, LBHS — because each does one thing well:

- **Moulton Meadows** is intimate, family-friendly, walkable for most of Laguna. Best for juniors learning to love the sport.
- **Alta Laguna** has the courts and space for camps and competitive UTR play. Best for tournaments and intensity.
- **LBHS** has the regulation courts for adult leagues, High Performance, and Cardio Tennis. Best for serious training.

We don't sprawl. Three locations, three lanes, one philosophy.

## What LBTA is not

- We are not a country club. No social membership tier.
- We are not a tennis franchise. We are founder-led and Laguna-rooted.
- We are not chasing volume. We grow by retention and referrals, not ads.
- We are not the cheapest. We are the most invested.
