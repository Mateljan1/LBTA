Our Founder

Founded by
Andrew Mateljan.

Fourth-generation tennis coach. Andrew was introduced to the game by his grandfather Steve at Ford Park in Redlands and has spent twenty years on tour and on court since. Top-12 nationally as a junior, two-time Kalamazoo Nationals competitor, ITF Futures circuit (US, Venezuela, El Salvador, Guatemala). Trained at Sánchez-Casal Spain under Jimmy Johnson. Seven years coaching internationally — Spain, Croatia, Norway — built the movement-first approach that runs every session at LBTA today.

Currently coaches Karue Sell (ATP #258 — took him from #860 to #258 in 2025). Colton Smith (ATP #133), Max McKennon (#458), and Ryan Seggerman (#63 in doubles) all came through the same training. Alex Michelsen came through the program at ages 12–13 — now ATP #30, 2022 Wimbledon Boys' Doubles champion.

"Structure creates confidence.
Confidence creates results."

The Three Pillars

Movement — the foundation. Footwork solves 80% of the problem before the swing happens.

Craft — structure that builds confidence. Consistent practice routines create lasting mental toughness.

Community — a community built on respect. Every LBTA session ends in a circle.
