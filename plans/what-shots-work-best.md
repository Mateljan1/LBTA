# What Shots Work Best — Pick From Your Library

Use this when you’re choosing from your existing photos and videos. It tells you **what to look for**, **what sells**, and **where each type works best** so you can find the right shot and drop it into the Desktop folder (then into the project).

---

## 1. Founder (Andrew) on the Homepage — Make It Convert

**Where it appears:** Main homepage, first section below the hero (“Founder” block). Large portrait, left side; copy and quote on the right. Also used on `/coaches` (FounderSection).

**Current:** `andrew-headshot.png` — 3:4 portrait, face/upper body.

### What works best for founder photos (what sells)

- **Approachable + authority:** You look like someone a parent or adult would trust and want to work with — warm but serious. Not stiff; not too casual.
- **Eye contact or near:** Either looking at camera (connection) or slightly off (thoughtful). Avoid looking down or away entirely.
- **On-court or courts visible:** Even a hint of court, net, or tennis in the frame signals “this is the coach.” Indoor/studio is OK if the vibe is warm and professional.
- **Lighting:** Natural or soft. Golden hour or overcast works well. Avoid harsh midday shadows or flat flash.
- **Crop:** 3:4 or 4:5 portrait (head to mid-chest or waist). Face in upper half so it stays visible on mobile. No need for full body.

### Shots to dig for (in order of impact)

1. **You on court, early morning or late afternoon** — clipboard or racquet optional; courts (or ocean) in background. Says “I’m here, I run this.”
2. **You with one junior or one adult** — side-by-side or you coaching. Says “I work with real players.”
3. **You at net or baseline** — teaching moment, not posed. Says “this is what happens at LBTA.”
4. **Clean portrait with court blur behind** — if you have a sharp portrait with bokeh courts, that’s strong for trust.

### What to avoid

- Formal suit/tie (feels corporate, not academy).
- Arms crossed (closed off).
- Only group shots where you’re small (founder block should be clearly you).

**Best single upgrade:** One **on-court portrait** (3:4 or 4:5) with natural light and a bit of court or sky — use it for both homepage founder block and `/coaches` FounderSection. Name it `andrew-portrait.webp` (or keep `andrew-headshot` and replace the file).

## 2. Pro Player Shots — What Gets Attention and Sells

**Where they appear:** Homepage “Results” full-bleed section (Karue); Success Stories; optional second “pro/college” strip.

### What sells most (in order)

1. **Intensity + focus** — Pro or college-age player in the middle of a drill or match. Concentration on face, body in motion. Says “we train serious players.”
2. **You (or coach) with the pro** — One clear shot of you coaching a known player. Credibility in one image.
3. **Match or tournament moment** — Point play, celebration, or pre-serve. Emotion + level of play.
4. **Before/after or progression** — If you have same player at different stages, that’s gold for Success Stories.

### Angles that work best

- **Medium shot (waist up)** for “results” hero — face visible, motion or tension in shoulders/arms.
- **Low angle (slightly from below)** for power and stature — use sparingly, one hero is enough.
- **Side or three-quarter** — racket in frame, ball or court visible. Tells a story.

### What to avoid

- Back of head only (no face = less connection).
- Crowded frame (one main subject reads best).
- Blurry or dark (we want “premium” not “phone snapshot”).

**Best 3 to gather:** (1) Karue — focused training or match moment. (2) One more pro or D1-level player — same vibe. (3) One shot of you with a pro/college player for “coach of champions” feel.

## 3. Junior Shots — What Parents Respond To

**Where they appear:** Homepage program cards (Juniors), Philosophy (Movement/Craft/Community), Community grid, Camps, Junior trial hero, Success Stories.

### What sells most (in order)

1. **Skill + joy** — Kid hitting well *and* smiling or engaged. Not grim concentration only; not goofing off. “They get better and they love it.”
2. **Coach–player connection** — You or another coach clearly teaching one kid or a small group. Trust and attention.
3. **Variety** — Mix of ages (little red ball, older green/yellow), boys and girls. “There’s a place for my child.”
4. **Progression** — Same kid in a progression (e.g. red → orange → green) if you have it; great for Success Stories.

### Shots to dig for

- **Junior girl hitting** — Forehand or backhand, good form, 10–16 age range. One clear “she could be my daughter” image.
- **Junior boy hitting** — Same idea; serve or groundstroke. One “he could be my son” image.
- **Little kids (red ball)** — Fun, low net, 4–8 age range. For camps/red ball card.
- **Orange / green ball** — 7–12 year olds in a clinic or rally. For camps and program cards.
- **Group celebration or high-five** — Belonging and team vibe for Philosophy “Community” and community grid.

### What to avoid

- Only one gender or one age (parents look for “someone like my kid”).
- Kids looking bored or frustrated.
- Empty courts with no players (we want life).

**Best 6 to gather:** One strong junior girl shot, one strong junior boy shot, one red-ball moment, one orange/green clinic, one group/celebration, one “coach and junior” moment. Use across Programs, Philosophy, Community, Camps.

## 4. Adult Shots — What Adult Visitors Respond To

**Where they appear:** Homepage Adult program card, Adult trial hero, Beginner program, Leagues/USTA/UTR, Match play, Why Choose, Community grid.

### What sells most (in order)

1. **“People like me”** — Adults 30–55, mixed gender, clearly having a good time or working on their game. Not only young athletes.
2. **Group vibe** — 4–8 adults in a clinic, Cardio Tennis, or social rally. Says “I won’t be alone; there’s a community.”
3. **Beginner-friendly** — Someone who looks like a rec player (not a pro) improving or enjoying a lesson. Reduces “I’m not good enough” fear.
4. **League / match play** — Doubles or team feel. For USTA/UTR and Match Play pages.

### Shots to dig for

- **Adult group lesson or clinic** — Coach in frame, 4–6 adults. Best for adult-trial hero or adult program card.
- **Adults in social rally or Cardio** — Energy, smiles, court in background.
- **One adult with coach** — Private-lesson feel for “Private” card or Why Choose.
- **League match or team huddle** — For leagues-hero and leagues card.
- **Mixed ages in one frame** — 30s–50s; shows range.

### What to avoid

- Only juniors in “adult” slots (adults need to see themselves).
- Intimidating “pro only” vibe on adult pages.
- Empty or sterile (we want warmth and activity).

**Best 5 to gather:** One strong adult-group hero (4–8 people), one adult + coach, one league/doubles moment, one Cardio/social moment, one “beginner” feel (lesson or first rally). Use for adult-trial, beginner-program, leagues, match-play, Why Choose.

## 6. Hero Video (Homepage) — What Gets Attention

**Where it appears:** Full-bleed at top of homepage (plays on load).

### What works best

- **Energy + place** — Motion (rally, drill, movement); courts and, if possible, a hint of Laguna (sky, horizon). 15–30 seconds.
- **Real people** — Juniors or adults or mix; not stock. Faces and action.
- **Sound (optional)** — Racket hits, atmosphere; or leave mute-friendly with strong visuals.
- **Opening frame** — Strong first frame (poster image) so before play it already looks premium.

### What to avoid

- Static or slow only (hero should feel alive).
- Too long (keep under 30 sec for LCP and attention).
- Generic or stock look.

**Best 1 to pick:** Your single best 15–30 sec clip that shows “LBTA in action” — training, rally, or clinic with real players and your courts. We’ll use it as hero and generate a poster frame.

## 7. Philosophy Pillars (3 Images) — Movement, Craft, Community

**Where they appear:** Homepage Philosophy section (3 squares); also Philosophy page.

### What to look for

- **Movement** — Footwork, agility, player in motion (junior or adult). Dynamic, not posed.
- **Craft** — Technique, structure, coach explaining or player in focused form. “Discipline” or “craft” vibe.
- **Community** — Group, team, high-fives, or mixed ages together. “Belonging.”

One strong image per pillar is enough. Square crop (1:1) works best.

---

## 8. Community Grid (6–12 Images) — Variety and Belonging

**Where they appear:** Homepage community grid (6 slots); can expand to 8–12 for “Recent moments” or more variety.

### What works best

- **Variety** — Different ages, genders, and moments: little kids, juniors, adults, coach, group, solo. So every visitor can think “that could be me.”
- **Real moments** — Candid or semi-candid: rally, laugh, high-five, lesson. Not only posed lineups.
- **Square crop** — 1:1 so the grid is even.

**Best 6 to start:** One little kid, one junior girl, one junior boy, one adult, one group, one coach-with-player. Then add more as you have them.

## 10. What Sells the Most (Order of Impact)

If you only have time to upgrade a few things, do this order:

1. **Founder photo** — One on-court, approachable, professional portrait of you. Replaces or upgrades current headshot. Big trust gain.
2. **Results section** — One killer pro/college shot (Karue or next best). Proves “we produce results.”
3. **Adult group hero** — One shot of 4–8 adults on court. Makes adult and beginner pages feel real.
4. **Why Choose** — One “coach on court,” one “facility/community.” Trust + place in one section.
5. **Junior + community variety** — So parents and adults see “someone like me” and “this is a real community.”

After that: philosophy pillars, program cards, community grid, then gallery and extra heroes. Video hero is high impact too — if you have one strong 15–30 sec clip, use it.
