Lane 01 · Today

Where the site is

Baseline observations from the repo, sitemap, and live pages.

  • 43 live routes31 in sitemap.ts · 12 additional (coach profiles, tools, admin, print, thank-you).
  • Single source of truth in /data/*.jsonSchedule, pricing, leagues, camps centralized. Good foundation — but only if everything downstream respects it.

  • HNW positioning is implicitFounder story and success arcs are strong; no explicit concierge / retreat / relocating-family path.

Lane 02 · Off

What the audit flags

Specific drift between site data, operations doc, and downstream surfaces.

  • Summer camp end date conflictspring-summer-2026.json (Aug 28) vs year2026.json (Aug 29).
  • Hardcoded dates in UIProgramsTabView.tsx renders "June 15 – August 28" — bypasses JSON.
  • Email narrative driftSummer plan email shows June 16 start vs JSON/doc June 15.
  • Duplicate pricing exposureAny price echoed inside a page component is a future drift risk — rule already says no hardcoded prices outside /data.
  • Social proof needs verificationGoogle rating count, named testimonials (e.g. "Sarah M.") should be auditable and permissioned.
  • UX: destination vs local is unclearA visiting parent has no obvious "Stay & Train" path; a local has no reassurance of priority.
  • External systems not auto-verifiedActiveCampaign, GHL, Playbypoint, city portal — only manually spot-checked today.
Lane 03 · Direction

Where we want to take it

Boutique academy for serious development + Laguna-as-destination, without losing community.

  • Two funnels, one brandLocal / recurring and Destination / concierge — shared voice, separate entry paths.
  • Truth is non-negotiableJSON → site → email → doc in one direction; grep for any literal date/price outside /data.
  • HNW voice = AUTHORITY + warmthSpecifics (method, ratio, pathway), not hype. No "elite / world-class / don't miss out."
  • Grand Slam trajectory framed as processHours, structure, tournament support, assessment — not trophy promises.
  • Destination hub + hotel pilotsOne repeatable template for a stay-and-train retreat; 1–2 named partners before scaling.
  • Simple professional saleEvery key screen: one primary CTA, one secondary. ≤3 clicks from home to book or inquire.
  • CRM-ready inquiriesTags for Local, Visitor, Trial, Camp, Retreat — so follow-up quality matches the positioning.

Why this matters for the HNW / destination strategy

Premium buyers detect inconsistency instantly. A parent who flies in because of a newsletter, then sees a different date on the website and a third date in the confirmation email, won't complain — they'll quietly choose a competitor. Fixing the truth layer before upgrading the marketing layer protects every dollar spent on partnerships, ads, or concierge outreach.

Where we're going

Three pillars that have to sit together

The moment any single pillar starts to dominate, the brand loses. All three have to feel true on the same site.

I

Local-first community

Families who live here get priority, recognition, and a standard that doesn't change when visitors show up.

  • Repeated community proof on home + programs.
  • One sentence on court priority where policy supports it.
  • Program pages link back to schedule truth, not sideways to marketing.
II

Boutique developmental

Serious players — including long-horizon Grand Slam trajectory — see a program that respects the process.

  • Method specificity: movement, structure, tournament support.
  • Individual readiness language, not trophy promises.
  • Named coaches, clear ratios, real alumni outcomes.
III

Laguna as destination

Summer camps, adult retreats, and pickleball + tennis packages make Laguna a reason to travel — on our terms.

  • Dedicated hub (route TBD) with inquiry-first CTAs.
  • 1–2 hotel pilots with written partnership standards.
  • Concierge form fields → CRM tags → human follow-up.
Sequence

Best move forward — in order

Trust first, positioning second, one thin increment third. This ordering makes every later move cheaper.

Execute the operational consistency audit (minimum viable)

Build the discrepancy log for every parent-visible surface: site JSON, homepage/FAQ, camp copy, top 2 emails. Close the known date/price conflicts. No premium messaging goes live until this is clean.

Loss function: trust in operational truth Owner: ops + web ~2 weeks

Lock the one-paragraph positioning

Who we are for locals vs visitors. What we never claim. How "Grand Slam / HNW" shows up in language (process, not trophies). No code; it unlocks every subsequent copy and IA decision.

Stakeholder alignment 1 session

Ship one thin increment — not a redesign

Pick one of: (a) destination hub stub with inquiry CTA, (b) concierge fields + CRM tags on existing forms, (c) sitemap + internal link clean-up. Validate with 3–5 real parents before building anything else.

Barbell: core stable + one bet 1–2 weeks dev

Partnerships after the above, never before

1–2 named hotel pilots, written standards, one repeatable landing pattern. Scale only when inbound inquiries land in CRM with clean tags and a human follow-up plan.

Conditional on steps 1–3

Compound the learnings

After each increment, capture: what messaging landed, what confused, which CRM paths worked. Feed back into plans/ and the memory system so the next cycle is faster than this one.

How to use this tracker

Each task's dot has three states you can flip by hand in the HTML: empty ring (not started), orange (class="task progress"), green check (class="task done"). Red (class="task risk") marks items that block everything downstream. When a phase hits 100%, the progress bar fills — that's the moment to run /compound:learn and lock the wins into memory before the next phase starts.
