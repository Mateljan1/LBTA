4 · Blog foundation — /blog scaffold + 5 launch posts

Multi-week Uncapped SEO

The only real long-tail SEO asset LBTA doesn't have yet

Currently zero blog → zero long-tail traffic. Every post after launch is a new ranking URL that compounds. More importantly: for an October 2027 buyer, "the site has a blog with traffic" is a concrete asset in the CIM (not the same thing as "the site has a blog").

Scope split

Phase A — scaffold only (~1 week of dev)
Goal: make /blog exist, render posts from MDX or a data-driven source, generate sitemap entries, emit Article schema. No posts yet.

Outputs:
• app/blog/page.tsx — blog index
• app/blog/[slug]/page.tsx — post template
• content/blog/*.mdx — post source
• lib/blog.ts — listing + front-matter parsing
• Article + BreadcrumbList JSON-LD schema per post
• RSS feed /blog/rss.xml
Phase B — 5 launch posts (~3–4 weeks content)
Five posts that answer real search queries, in the LBTA calm-authority voice:
• "How to Choose a Summer Tennis Camp" — long-tail: "tennis camp choosing [age]"
• "Movement Before Strokes — Why Footwork Comes First" — methodology authority
• "Understanding UTR — What the Number Really Measures" — educational, high search volume
• "College Tennis: The Four Years That Actually Matter" — parent-intent, high UTR fee tier
• "Private vs Group Lessons — Which Works for Your Family" — comparison query (money keyword)

~1500 words each. Andrew drafts from voice memo; I turn into publish-ready MDX with frontmatter + OG image.

Why not more posts at launch?

Five high-quality posts with internal cross-links and strong titles outrank twenty thin posts. Google's threshold is content depth; readers' threshold is "does this teach me something I didn't know?" — also depth. After launch, cadence = one post every 2 weeks.

Buyer-facing footprint

CIM line in the exit-playbook asset inventory becomes: "Blog with N original long-form posts, Article + BreadcrumbList schema, organic search traffic for [top 3 keywords]." — specific, measurable, transferable. A blog that's just a CMS with zero posts doesn't earn that line.
